SPEED, JOHN, an English historian, was born at Farington, in Cheshire, in the year 1542. He was by profession a taylor, and freeman of the company of merchant tailors in the city of London. In 1606, he published his Theatre of Great Britain, which was afterwards reprinted in folio, under the title of the Theatre of the Empire of Great Britain. His Genealogies of Scripture were first bound up with the Bible in 1614, when the first edition of the present translation was printed. In 1614 appeared his History of Great Britain, which has been translated into Latin; and in 1616 he published his Cloud of Witnesses, in octavo. He lived in marriage 57 years with his wife, by whom he had twelve sons and six daughters; and died in 1629. He was interred in the church of St Giles's, Cripplegate, London, where a monument was erected to his memory.
