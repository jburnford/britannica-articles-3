STIPULA

article · 297 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

STIPULA, in Botany, one of the fulcra or props of plants, defined by Linnaeus to be a scale, or small leaf, stationed on each side the base of the footstalks of the flower and leaves, at their first appearance, for the purpose of support. Eimgren restricts it to the footstalks of the leaves only.
