STADIUM

article · 458 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

STADIUM, an ancient Greek long measure, containing 125 geometrical paces, or 625 Roman feet, corresponding to our furlong. The word is said to be formed from the Greek word stasis "a station," or stasis "to stand," because it is reported that Hercules having run a stadium at one breath, stood still at the end of it. The Greeks usually measured distances by stadia, which they called stadion. Stadium also signified the course on which their races were run.
