STRETCHING

article · 410 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

STRETCHING, in Navigation, is generally understood to imply the progression of a ship under a great surface of sail, when close-hauled. The difference between this term and standing, consists apparently in the quantity of sail; which in the latter may be very moderate; but stretching generally signifies excess; as, we saw the enemy at daybreak stretching to the southward under a crowd of sail, &c. Falconer.
