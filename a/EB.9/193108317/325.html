SPINOSUS CAULIS

article · 311 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

SPINOSUS CAULIS, in Botany, a stem covered with strong woody prickles, whose roots are not superficial, but proceeding from the body of the stem. When applied to a leaf, spinosum folium, it indicates the margin running out into rigid points or prickles, quod margine exit in acuminis duriora, rigida, pungentia.
