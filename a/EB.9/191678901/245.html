TRANSMUTATION

TRANSMUTATION, in alchemy, denotes the act of changing imperfect metals into gold or silver. This is also called the grand operation; and, they say, it is to be effected with the philosopher's stone.

The trick of transmuting cinnabar into silver is thus: the cinnabar, being bruised grossly, is stratified in a crucible with granulated silver, and the crucible placed in a great fire; and, after due time for calcination, taken off; then the matter, being poured out, is found to be cinnabar.
