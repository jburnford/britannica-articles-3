WALLACE, SIR WILLIAM, a gallant general of the Scots, who endeavoured to rescue his country from the English yoke; but being taken prisoner, he was unjustly tried by the English laws, condemned, and executed as a traitor to Edward I. in 1304. See SCOTLAND, no 103, et seq.
