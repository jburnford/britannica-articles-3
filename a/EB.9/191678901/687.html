Cross-references: MORAL PHILOSOPHY

VIRTUE, a term used in various significations. In the general it denotes power, or the perfection of any thing, whether natural or supernatural, animate, or inanimate, essential or accessory. But, in its more proper or restrained sense, virtue signifies a habit, which improves and perfects the possessor and his actions. See MORAL PHILOSOPHY, No 84.
