WINCHELSEA

WINCHELSEA, Anne Countess of, a lady of excellent genius, especially in poetry, was maid of honour to the duchess of York, second wife to King James II. and was afterwards married to Henage, second son of the earl of Winchelsea. One of the most considerable of the countess of Winchelsea's poems was that on the

Winchester, Spleen. A collection of her poems was printed at London in 1713, containing a tragedy never acted, intitled Ariflomenes. The countess died in 1720 without issue, as her husband did in 1726.
