UMBELLATÆ, the name of a class in Ray's and Vol. XX. Part II.

Tournefort's methods, consisting of plants whose flowers grow in umbels, with five petals that are often unequal, and two naked seeds that are joined at top and separated below.

The same plants constitute the 43th order of Linnaeus's Fragments of a Natural Method. See BOTANY.
