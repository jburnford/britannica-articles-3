VASSAL

article · 442 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Cross-references: FEODAL

VASSAL, in our ancient customs, signified a tenant or feudatory; or person who vowed fidelity and homage to a lord, on account of some land, &c. held of him in fee; also a slave or servant, and especially a domestic of a prince.—Vassallus is said to be quasi inferior socius; as the vassal is inferior to his master, and must serve him; and yet he is in a manner his companion, because each of them is obliged to the other.
