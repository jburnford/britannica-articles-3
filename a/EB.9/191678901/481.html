VADIUM Vivum

sub_entry · 484 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Cross-references: MORTGAGE

VADIUM Vivum, or Living Pledge, is when a man borrows a sum (suppose 200l.) of another; and grants him an estate, as of 20l. per annum, to hold till the rents and profits shall repay the sum so borrowed. This is an estate conditioned to be void as soon as such sum is raised. And in this case the land or pledge is said to be living: it subsists, and survives the debts; and, immediately on the discharge of that, reverts to the borrower.

Vadium Mortuum, or Dead Pledge. See MORTGAGE.
