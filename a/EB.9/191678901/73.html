TIBUR, in Ancient Geography, a town of Latium, pleasantly situated on the Anio. Here Horace had his villa and house; and here he wished to end his days. Here Adrian built an extraordinary villa called Tiburina, inscribed with the names of the provinces and of the most considerable places, (Spartian); near which Zenobia had a house called Zenobia, (Trebellius, Pollio). Hither Augustus often retreated on account of its salubrity, (Suetonius): for which it is greatly recommended, (Martial). Anciently, when the Romans had far extended their territory, it was the utmost place of banishment, (Ovid). It had a temple of Hercules; and therefore called Herculeum. In the temple was a library, (A. Gellius). Now Tivoli in the Campagna di Roma, on the Tevere.
