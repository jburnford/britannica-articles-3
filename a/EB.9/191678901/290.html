TRES TABERNÆ

article · 304 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

TRES TABERNÆ, in Ancient Geography, a place in Latium, lying on the Via Appia, on the left or south side of the river Alura, to the north of the Paludes Pomptinæ. Its ruins are now seen near Cisterna, a village in the Campagna di Roma, 21 miles from Rome, whence the Christians went out to meet St. Paul.
