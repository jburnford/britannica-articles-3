COTICE, or COTISE

sub_entry · 360 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

COTICE, or COTISE, in Heraldry, is the fourth part of the bend; which with us is seldom or ever borne but in couples, with a bend between them; whence probably the name; from the French cote, "side;" they being borne, as it were, a-side of the bend.—A bend thus bordered is said to be cotised, cotice. He bears sable on a bend cotised argent three cinquefoils.
