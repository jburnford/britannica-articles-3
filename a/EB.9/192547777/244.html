CONTOURNIATED

article · 397 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CONTOURNIATED, a term among antiquaries applied to medals, the edges of which appear as if turned in a lathe. This sort of work seems to have had its origin in Greece; and to have been designed to perpetuate the memories of great men, particularly those who had borne away the prize at the solemn games. Such are those remaining of Homer, Solon, Euclid, Pythagoras, Socrates, and several athletes.
