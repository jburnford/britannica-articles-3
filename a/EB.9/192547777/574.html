COSHERING

article · 331 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Cross-references: GEOMETRY

COSHERING, in the feudal customs, a kind of right of the lords to lie and feall themselves and their followers at their tenants houses. The word coshering may perhaps be derived from the old English word cosbe, a cot or cottage.

CO-SINE, in Trigonometry, the sine of an arch which is the complement of another to 90°. See GEOMETRY.
