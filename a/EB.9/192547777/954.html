CROWNING

article · 370 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CROWNING, in Architecture, is understood, in the general, of any thing that terminates or finishes a member or decoration. Thus, a cornice, a pediment, &c. are called crownings. Thus also the abacus is said to crown the capital; and thus any member or moulding is said to be crowned when it has a fillet over it; and a niche is crowned when it is covered with a capital.
