COROLLISTÆ

article · 431 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

COROLLISTÆ, a name by which Linnaeus distinguishes those systematic botanists who have arranged vegetables from the regularity, figure, number, and other circumstances, of the petals, or beautiful coloured leaves of the flowers. The best systems of this kind are those of Rivinus and Tournefort. The former proceeds upon the regularity and number of the petals; the latter, with much more certainty, on their regularity and figure.
