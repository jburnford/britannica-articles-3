CONCOU

article · 280 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CONCOU, in Botany, a name given by the people of Guinea to an herb, which is a great esteem among them for killing that troublesome sort of worm called the Guinea-worm, that breeds in their flesh. They bruise the leaves, and mixing them with oil apply them in form of a cataplasm.
