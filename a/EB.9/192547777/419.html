CORINNA

article · 448 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CORINNA, a Grecian lady, celebrated for her beauty and poetic talents, was born at Thessu, a city of Beotia, and was the disciple of Myrtis another Grecian lady. Her verses were so esteemed by the Greeks, that they gave her the name of the lyric muse. She lived in the time of Pindar, about 495 years before Christ; and is said to have gained the prize of lyric poetry from that poet; but Paufanias observes that her beauty made the judges partial.
