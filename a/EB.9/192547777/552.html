CORYCOMACHIA

article · 283 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CORYCOMACHIA, among the ancients, was a sort of exercise in which they pushed forwards a ball,

suspended from the ceiling, and at its return either caught it with their hands, or suffered it to meet their body. Oribasus informs us it was recommended for extenuating too gross bodies.
