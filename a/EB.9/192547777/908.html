CRONIUS, in Chronology, the ancient name of the Athenian month Hecatombicon; which was the first

Cronslot, first of their year, and answered to the latter part of our June and beginning of July.—There were feasts called Cronioner celebrated at Athens in this month, in honour of Saturn, answering to the Saturnalia of the Romans.
