CRANK, in sea-language. A ship is said to be crank-fided, when for want of a sufficient quantity of ballast or cargo, she cannot bear her sails, or can bear but small sail for fear of oversetting.—She is said to be crank by the ground, when her floor is so narrow that she cannot be brought on ground without danger.
