CONTRITION

article · 329 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CONTRITION, in Theology, a sorrow for our sins, resulting from the reflection of having offended God, from the sole consideration of his goodness, without any regard to the punishment due to the trespass, and attended with a sincere resolution of forsaking them. The word is derived from the Latin contritere, to break or bruise.
