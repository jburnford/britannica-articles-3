CROWNING

article · 281 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CROWNING, in sea-language, denotes the finishing part of a knot made at the end of a rope. It is performed by interweaving the ends of the different strands artfully amongst each other, so as that they may not become loosened or untwisted. They are useful in all kinds of stoppers.
