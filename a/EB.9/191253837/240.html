PACHAMAC

PACHAMAC, a valley of Peru, in South America, ten miles south of Lima; celebrated for its pleasantness and fertility, but more on account of a magnificent temple built by the Incas of Peru, to the honour of their god. When the Spaniards conquered Peru, they found immense riches therein.
