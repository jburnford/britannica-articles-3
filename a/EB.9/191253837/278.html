PAITA, a sea-port of America, in Peru, and in the audience of Quito. The town consists of about 200 houses but one story high; and the walls are made of split cane and mud, and the roofs only a covering of leaves. The only defence of Paita is a fort without either ditch or outwork; but it is surrounded by a brick wall of little or no strength, on which are mounted eight pieces of cannon. It was frequently plundered by the bucaniers; and Commodore Anson got possession of its fort in 1741, and took and burnt the town because the governor refused to ransom it. W. Long. 81. 19. S. Lat. 6. 12.
