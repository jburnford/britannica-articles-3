ORLEANOIS

ORLEANOIS, a province of France, now forming the department of Loiret, and including the several districts of Orleans-Proper, Beauce-Proper or Chartre, Dunois, Vendomois, Blaisois, the greatest part of Gâtinois, and Perche-Gouet. The principal rivers of it are the Loire, the Loiret, the Cher, the Laconie, the Aigle, the Hyere, the Yonne, and the Eyre. There are also some remarkable canals, particularly those of Briare and Orleans. The river Loire, and the canals drawn from thence, greatly facilitate and promote the inland trade of the kingdom, and particularly of this department.

Orleans, in Latin Aurelianensis Ager, is bounded on the south by Sologne, on the north by Upper-Beauce, on the east by Gâtinois, and on the west by Dunois and Vendomois. The Loire divides it into Upper and Lower; the former lying to the north, and the latter to the south of that river. It yields plenty of grain, wine, wood, and fruit, and abounds in cattle, game, and fish.
