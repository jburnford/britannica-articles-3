ORRERY, a curious machine for representing the motions or phases of the heavenly bodies. See ASTRONOMY, p. 171.

The reason of its being called an Ortery was this: Mr Rowley, a mathematical instrument maker, having got one from Mr George Graham, the original inventor, to be sent abroad with some of his own instruments, he copied it, and made the first for the earl of Ortery. Sir Richard Steele, who knew nothing of Mr Graham's machines, thinking to do justice to the first encourager, as well as to the inventor of such a curious instrument, called it an Ortery, and gave Mr Rowley the praise due to Mr Graham.
