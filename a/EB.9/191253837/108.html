ORTYGIA

article · 940 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Cross-references: EPHESUS

ORTYGIA, the birthplace of Diana, was a beautiful grove of trees of various kinds, chiefly cypress, near Ephesus; on the coast, a little up from the sea. This place was filled with shrubs and images. The priests of the goddess were cunuchs, and exceedingly respected by the people. A general assembly was held there yearly, and splendid entertainments were provided, and mystic sacrifices solemnized. The Cenchrus, probably a crooked river, ran through it; and above it was the mountain Solmifus, on which it was fabled, the Curetes stood, and rattled on their shields, to divert the attention of Juno. The improved face of a country is perishable like human beauty. Not only the birthplace of Diana and its sanctity are forgotten, but the grove and buildings which adorned it appear no more; and perhaps, says Dr Chandler, the land has encroached on the sea, and the valley, in which Arvia is, was once Ortygia.
