PACTOLUS

PACTOLUS, in Ancient Geography, a river of Lydia, called Chrysorrhoas, from its rolling down golden sand, according to Herodotus, Plutarch, Pliny, and Strabo; rising in Mount Tmolus (Strabo). From this river Cretus is thought to have had all his riches. In Strabo's time it ceased to roll down any. It ran through Sardis; after which it fell into the Hermus, and both together into the Ægean sea at Phocæa in Ionia. A river celebrated by Virgil, Ovid, Lucan, Lycophon, Horace, Appollonius.
