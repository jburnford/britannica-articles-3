ARATUS

article · 647 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ARATUS, general of the Acheans, conquered Niocles tyrant of Sicyon. Two years after he surprised the castle called Acrocorinthus, and drove out the king of Macedonia: he delivered Argos from its tyrants, and was poisoned by Philip II. king of Macedonia, whom he had newly restored. He was about 62 when he died, the second year of the 141st Olympiad. He was interred at Sicyon, and received the greatest honours from his countrymen. His son, who had also been praetor, was poisoned by King Philip. Polybius gives us so great a character of Aratus the father's Commentaries or History, that the loss of so valuable a work is highly to be regretted.
