ANTI

article · 486 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ANTI, in matters of Literature, is a title given to divers pieces written by way of answer to others, whose names are usually annexed to the anti. See the Anti of M. Baillet; and the Anti-Baillet of M. Menage: there are also Anti-Menagiani, &c. Cæsar the dictator wrote two books by way of answer to what had been objected to him by Cato, which he called Anti-Catones; these are mentioned by Juvenal, Cicero, &c. Vives assures us, he had seen Cæsar's Anti-Catones in an ancient library.
