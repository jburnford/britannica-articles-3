ARAHUM

article · 278 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ARAHUM, or HAKAHUM, in ancient writers, denotes a place consecrated or set apart for holy purposes. Hence the phrase in arabo jurare, or conjurare, "to make oath in the church;" because, by the Riparian laws, all oaths were to be taken in the church on the relics of the saints.
