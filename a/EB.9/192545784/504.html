AQUINO

AQUINO, PHILIP D', in Latin Aquinas or Aquinus, having turned from Judaism, had a pension from the clergy of France; and acquired much reputation by his knowledge of the Hebrew language, which he taught at Paris, in the reign of Louis XIII. and by the books he published, among which is his Dictionarium Hebraico-Chaldaico-Thalmadico-Rabbinicum. His grandson, Anthony D'Aquin, was first physician to Louis XIV.
