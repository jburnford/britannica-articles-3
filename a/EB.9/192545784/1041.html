ASCETERIUM

article · 310 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ASCETERIUM, in Ecclesiastical Writers, is frequently used for a monastery, or place set apart for the exercise of virtue and religion. The word is formed from ascesis "exercise;" or ascetra, "one who performs exercise." Originally it signified a place where the athlete or gladiators performed their exercises.
