AREMBERG

article · 337 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

AREMBERG, a small town of Germany, in the circle of Weithalia, defended by a castle. It is the capital of a county of the same name, and was erected into a principality by the emperor Maximilian II. in favour of John de Ligne, lord of Barbazon, who took the name of Aremberg. It is seated on the river Aër, E. Long. 7. 3. N. Lat. 50. 27.
