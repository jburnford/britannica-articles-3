ASKERON

article · 356 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ASKERON, a place five miles from Doncaster, noted for a medicinal spring. It is a strong sulphureous water, and is slightly impregnated with a purging salt. It is recommended internally and externally in strumous and other ulcers, scabs, leprosy, and similar complaints. It is good in chronic obstructions, and in cases of worms and foulness of the bowels.
