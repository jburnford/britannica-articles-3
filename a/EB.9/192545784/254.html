APAULIA

article · 519 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

APAULIA, in Antiquity, the third day of a marriage solemnity. It was thus called, because the bride, returning to her father's house, did anapaulai te pateris, lodge apart from the bridegroom. Some will have the apaulia to have been the second day of the marriage, viz. that whereon the chief ceremony was performed; thus called by way of contradistinction from the first day, which was called peranai.
