ANXUR

ANXUR, in Ancient Geography, a city of the Volscii, in Latium; called Tarracina, by the Greeks and Latins: Now Tarracina; situated on an eminence (Livy, Horace, Sil. Italicus). Anxur, a citizen of Auxur (Livy). And the epithet Anxurus, a name of Jupiter, worshipped without a beard at Auxur (Virgil). Though others read Anxur, or Axyrus, without a razor. E. Long. 14. 5. Lat. 41. 18.
