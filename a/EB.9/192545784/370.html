APORIA

article · 447 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

APORIA, is a figure in Rhetoric, by which the speaker shows, that he doubts where to begin for the multitude of matter, or what to say in some strange and ambiguous thing; and doth, as it were, argue the case with himself. Thus Cicero says, Whether he took them from his fellows more impudently, gave them to a harlot more lasciviously, removed them from the Roman people

more wickedly, or altered them more presumptuously, I can not well declare.
