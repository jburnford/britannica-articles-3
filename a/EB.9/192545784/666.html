ARDRAH, a small territory or kingdom of Africa, in Guinea properly so called. It lies at the bottom of the gulf of St. Thomas, and has a town called Ardrak, supposed to be the capital. The inhabitants are very licentious, and have neither temple nor any place for religious worship. However they are very courageous;

and their king was absolute till lately that the king of Dahomy made war upon this and the neighbouring territories, brought them under subjection, and burnt the towns, particularly Ardrak. The air is very unwholesome to Europeans: yet the natives live to a great age; but the smallpox makes great destruction among them. This country is fertile in Indian corn, palm wine, plants, and fruits, which last all the year; and they make a great deal of salt.
