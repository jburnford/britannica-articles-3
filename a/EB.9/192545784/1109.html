ASPATICUM

article · 413 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ASPATICUM, (from ασπάζεσθαι, "I salute,") in ecclesiastical writers, a place, or apartment, adjoining to the ancient churches, wherein the bishop and presbyters sat, to receive the salutations of the persons who came to visit them, desire their blessing, or consult them on business.—This is also called aspaticum diaconicum, receptorium, metatorium, or mesatorium, and salutatorium: in English, "greeting-house."
