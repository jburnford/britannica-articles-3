MELETIANS

article · 633 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MELETIANS, in ecclesiastical history, the name of a considerable party who adhered to the cause of Meletius bishop of Lycopolis, in Upper Egypt, after he was deposed, about the year 306, by Peter bishop of Alexandria, under the charge of his having sacrificed to the gods, and having been guilty of other heinous crimes; though Epiphanius makes his only failing to have been an excessive severity against the lapid. This dispute, which was at first a personal difference between Meletius and Peter, became a religious controversy; and the Meletian party subsisted in the fifth century, but was condemned by the first council of Nice.
