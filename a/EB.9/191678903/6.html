MEDIETAS LINGUÆ

article · 699 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MEDIETAS LINGUÆ, in Law, signifies a jury, or inquest impanelled, of which the one half are natives of this land and the other foreigners. This jury is never used except where one of the parties in a plea is a stranger and the other a denizen. In petit treason, murder,

Medimnus murder, and felony, foreigners are allowed this privilege; but not in high treason, because an alien in that case shall be tried according to the rules of the common law, and not by a medietas linguae. A grand jury ought not in any case to be of a medietas linguae; and the person that would have the advantage of a trial in this way, is to pray the same, otherwise it will not be permitted on a challenge of the jurors.
