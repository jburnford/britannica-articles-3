METROPOLIS

METROPOLIS, in Ancient Geography, a town of Acarnania, a little to the south of Stratos.—Another, of Lydia; situated between Colophon and Priene, near the Cayster.—A third, of Phrygia; sacred to the mother of the gods, who was here worshipped.—A fourth Metropolis of Esiotis, a district in Thessaly, to the east of Gomphi, and the last town of that district. Metropolis, the people.
