MEGARIS

article · 492 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MEGARIS, in Ancient Geography, the country of the Megareans, is described as a rough region, like Attica; the mountain called Oncian or the Asinine, now Macriplagi or "the long Mountain," extending through it towards Boeotia and Mount Citheron. It belonged

to Ionia or Attica, until it was taken by the Peloponnesians in the reign of Codrus, when a colony of Dorians settled in it. This territory had Attica to the east, Boeotia to the north and west, and the isthmus of Corinth to the south.
