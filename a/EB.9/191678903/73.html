MELIBOEIA

article · 276 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MELIBOEIA, in Ancient Geography, an island of Syria, at the mouth of the Orontes; which, before it falls into the sea, forms a spreading lake round it. This island was famous for its purple dye. Thought to be a colony of Thessalians; and hence Lucretius's epithet, Thessalica.
