MERCY-SEAT

article · 684 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MERCY-SEAT, or PROFITIATORY, in Jewish antiquity, the covering of the ark of the covenant.—The Hebrew name of this cover, which we translate mercy-seat, is Capporeth (Exod. xxv. 17, 22.), from Cappor, which signifies to cover, to shut up, to expiate, to pay. This cover was of gold, and at its two ends were fixed the two cherubim of the same metal, which by their wings extended forwards, seemed to form a throne for the majesty of God, who in scripture is represented to us as sitting between the cherubim, and the ark itself was as it were his footstool. It was from hence that God gave his oracles to Moses, or to the high priest that consulted him, Exod. xxv. 22. Numb. vii. 89.)
