GARUMNA, a noble and navigable river of Gaul, which rising from the Pyrenees, formerly bounded Aquitain on the north (Cæsar); but by the new regulation of Augustus divided it in the middle, emptying itself to the north of Burdegala, in the Aquitanic ocean. Now the Garonne.
