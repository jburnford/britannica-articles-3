GORDIANUS I

Cross-references: ROME

GORDIANUS I. (a Roman general), was for his valour and virtues chosen emperor by the army in the reign of Maximinus, A. D. 237; but his son, whom he had associated with himself in the throne, being slain by Capellian, the governor of Mauritania for Maximinus,

Gordianus Maximinus, Gordianus killed himself the same year. See ROME.
