GIBLETS

article · 307 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

GIBLETS, the offals or entrails of a goose; including the heart and liver, with the feet, gizzard, &c. The word is supposed to be formed of goblets; from the French gobeau, "mouthful."—Giblets make a considerable article in cookery: they boil giblets, stew giblets, make ragouts of giblets, giblet pies, &c.
