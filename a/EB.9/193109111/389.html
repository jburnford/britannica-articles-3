GLATZ

article · 430 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

GLATZ, a strong town of Bohemia, capital of a county of the same name, seated on the river Neisse; and well fortified with a castle. The county was ceded to the king of Prussia by the queen of Hungary in 1742; and is about 45 miles in length, and 25 in breadth. It has mines of pit coal, silver, and iron; good quarries, plenty of cattle, and fine springs of mineral water. The town is situated in E. Long. 15. 16. N. Lat. 50. 25.
