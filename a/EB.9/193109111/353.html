GIRT

article · 665 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

GIRT, the situation of a ship which is moored so straight by her cables, extending from the hawse to two distant anchors, as to be prevented from swinging or turning about according to any change of the wind or tide, to the current of which her head would otherwise be directed. The cables are extended in this manner, by a strong application of mechanical powers within the ship; so that when the veers, or endeavours to swing about, her side bears upon one of the cables, which catches on her heel, and interrupts her in the act of traversing. In this position she must ride with her broadside to the wind or current, till one or both of the cables are slackened.
