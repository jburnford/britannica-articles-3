SECANT

SECANT, in Geometry, a line that cuts another or divides it into parts. The secant of a circle is a line drawn from the circumference on one side to a point without the circumference on the other; and it is demonstrated by geometers, that of several secants drawn to the same point, that is the longest which passes through the centre of the circle. The portions, however, of these several secants that are without the circle are so much the greater as they recede from the centre, and the least external portion is of that secant which passes through it.
