SEPTENNIAL Elections

sub_entry · 697 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

SEPTENNIAL Elections. Blackstone, in his Commentaries, vol. i. p. 189. says, (after observing that the utmost extent of time allowed the same parliament to sit by the stat. G. W. and M. c. 2. was three years), "But, by the statute 1 Geo. I. st. 1. c. 38. (in order prophesedly to prevent the great and continued expenses of frequent elections, and the violent heats and animosities consequent thereon, and for the peace and security of the government, just then recovering from the late rebellion), this term was prolonged to seven years; and what alone is an instance of the vast authority of parliament, the very same house that was chosen for three years enacted its own continuance for seven."
