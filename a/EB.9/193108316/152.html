SEIGNORAGE

article · 614 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

SEIGNORAGE, is a royalty or prerogative of the king, whereby he claims an allowance of gold and silver brought in the mias to be exchanged for coin. As seignorage, put of every pound weight of gold, the king had for his coin 5s. of which he paid to the master of the mint sometimes 1s. and sometimes 1s. 6d. Upon every pound weight of silver, the seignorage answered to the king in the time of Edward III. was 18. pennyweights, which then amounted to about 1s. out of which he sometimes paid 8d. at others 9d. to the master. In the reign of King Henry V. the king's seignorage of every pound of silver was 15d. &c.
