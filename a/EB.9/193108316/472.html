SHIFTING A TACKLE

article · 502 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

SHIFTING A TACKLE, in sea-language, the act of removing the blocks of a tackle to a greater distance from each other, on the object to which they are applied, in order to give a greater scope or extent to their purchase. This operation is otherwise called steering. Shifting the helm denotes the alteration of its position, by

Shilling. by pushing it towards the opposite side of the ship. Shifting the voyal, signifies changing its position on the capstern, from the right to the left, and vice versa.
