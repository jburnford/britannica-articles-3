EXAMPLE

article · 391 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

EXAMPLE, in Rhetoric, denotes an imperfect kind of induction or argumentation; whereby it is proved, that a thing which happened on some other occasion will happen again on the present one, from the similitude of the cases. As, "The war of the Thebans, against their neighbours the Phocians, was ruinous; consequently, that of the Athenians against their neighbours, will likewise be fatal."
