EXPERIENCE

article · 686 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

EXPERIENCE, a kind of knowledge acquired by long use without any teacher. It consists in the ideas of things we have seen or read, which the judgement has reflected on, to form for itself a rule or method.

Authors make three kinds of experience: The first is the simple uses of the external senses, whereby we perceive the phenomena of natural things without any direct attention thereto, or making any application thereof. The second is, when we premeditatively and designedly make trials of various things, or observe those done by others, attending closely to all effects and circumstances.
