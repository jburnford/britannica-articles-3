EPITRITUS

article · 388 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

EPITRITUS, in prosody, a foot consisting of three long syllables and one short. Of these, grammarians reckon four kinds: the first consisting of an iambus and spondee, as salutantes; the second, of a trocheus and spondee, as concitati; the third of a spondee, and an iambus, as communicans; and the fourth, of a spondee and trocheus, as incantare. See the articles SPONDEUS, TROCHEUS, &c.
