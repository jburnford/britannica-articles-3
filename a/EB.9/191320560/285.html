EPICOENE

article · 496 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

EPICOENE, in Grammar, a term applied to nouns, which, under the same gender and termination, mark indifferently the male and female species. Such in Latin is agula, vespertilio, &c. which signify equally a male or female eagle or bat.

Grammarians distinguish between epicene and common. A noun is said to be common of two kinds, when it may be joined either with a masculine or a feminine article; and epicene, when it is always joined to some of the two articles, and yet signifies both genders.
