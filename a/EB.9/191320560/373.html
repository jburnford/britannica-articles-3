EQUESTRIAN Order

sub_entry · 325 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

EQUESTRIAN Order, among the Romans, signified their

Equangu their knights or equites; as also their troopers or horsemen in the field; the first of which orders stood in contradistinction to the senators; at the last did to the foot, military, or infantry. Each of these distinctions was introduced into the state by Romulus.
