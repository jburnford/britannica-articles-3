EMBOLEMUS

article · 431 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

EMBOLEMUS, Ἐμβόλεμος, in Chronology, signifies "intercalation." The word is formed of ἔμβολος, "to insert."

As the Greeks made use of the lunar year, which is only 354 days; in order to bring it to the solar, which is 365 years, they had every two or three years an embolism, i. e. they added a 13th lunar month every two or three years, which additional month they called embolismus, ἔμβολισμός, because inserted, or intercalated.
