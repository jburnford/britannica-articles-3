EXAGGERATION

article · 363 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

EXAGGERATION, in Painting, a method by which the artist, in representing things, changes them too much, or makes them too strong, either in respect of the design or colouring. It differs from caricaturing, in that the latter perverts or gives a turn to the features of a face, &c. which they had not, whereas exaggeration only heightens or improves what they had.
