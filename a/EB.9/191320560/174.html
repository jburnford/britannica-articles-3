ENCYCLOPÆDIA

article · 651 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ENCYCLOPÆDIA, a term nearly synonymous with CYCLOPÆDIA; but adopted in preference to it in denominating the present work, as being more definite and of better authority. According to an observation of the late learned printer Mr Bower, the preposition EN makes the meaning of the word more precise. For Cyclopædia may denote "the instruction of a circle," as Cyclopædia is "the instruction of Cyrus," whereas in Encyclopædia the preposition determines the word to be from the dative of cyclos, "instruction in a circle." And Vossius, in his book De vitis sermonis, has observed, "That Cyclopædia is used by some authors, but Encyclopædia by the best."
