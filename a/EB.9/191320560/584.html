EUNAPIUS

article · 560 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

EUNAPIUS, a native of Sardis in Lydia, a celebrated sophist, physician, and historian, who flourished in the 4th century, under the emperors Valentinian, Valens, and Gratian. He wrote "The Lives of the Philosophers and Sophists," in which he frequently shows himself a bitter enemy to the Christians: also a "History of the Cæsars," which he deduced from the reign of Claudius where Herodian left off, down to that of Arcadius and Honorius. The history is lost; but we have the substance of it in Zosimus, who is supposed to have done little more than copy it.
