ROVERE

ROVERE, or ROVEREDO, a strong town of the Tyrol, on the confines of the republic of Venice; seated on the river Adige, at the foot of a mountain, and on the side of a stream, over which there is a bridge, defended by two large towers and a strong castle, 10 miles

south of Trent. The town is tolerably well built, and governed by a chief magistrate, styled a podestat. There are several churches and convents, that contain nothing worthy of notice. The most remarkable thing, and what they call the great wonder of Roveredo, is its spinning-house for a manufacture of silk, in which they have a great trade here to the fairs of Bolzano. They have also a very good trade in wine. Between Trent and Roveredo is the strong fort of Belem, belonging to the house of Austria. It is situated on a rock, and commands the roads at the foot of the mountain. E. Long. 11. 1. N. Lat. 46. 12.
