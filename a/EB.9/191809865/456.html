AXAYACATL

article · 535 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

AXAYACATL, the name of a species of fly, common in Mexico, about the lake; the eggs of which being deposited in immense quantities, upon the rushes and cora-flags, form large masses, which are taken up by fishermen and carried to market for sale. This caviare, called abuabiti, which has much the same taste with the caviare of fish, used to be eaten by the Mexicans, and is now a common dish among the

Spaniards. The Mexicans eat not only the eggs, but the flies themselves, made up together into a mass, and prepared with salt-petre.
