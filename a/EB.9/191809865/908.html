BARBARUS, Daniel

sub_entry · 461 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BARBARUS, Daniel, of the same family with the preceding, was patriarch of Aquileia, and famous for his learning. He was ambassador from Venice to England; 1 Barbary. land; and was one of the fathers of the council of Trent, where he acted with great zeal for the interest of the pope. He wrote, 1. A commentary upon Vitruvius. 2. Catena Graecorum Patrum in quinquaginta Psalmos Latine versa. 3. La Pratica della Prospettiva. He died in 1569, at 41 years of age.
