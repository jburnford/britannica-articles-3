ATHANOR. Chemists have distinguished by this name a furnace so constructed that it can always maintain an equal heat, and which shall last a long time without addition of fresh fuel.

The body of the athanor has nothing in it particular, and is constructed like ordinary furnaces. But at one of its sides, or its middle, there is an upright hollow tower, which communicates with the fireplace by one or more sloping openings, and which has a lid to close its upper opening. This furnace is now rarely used.
