AUREUS MONT

article · 355 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

AUREUS MONT, in Ancient Geography, a mountain in the north-west of Corsica, whose ridge runs out to the north-east and south-east, forming an elbow.—Another mountain of Moesia Superior, or Servia (Peutinger), to the south of the Danube, with a cognominal town at its foot on the same river. The emperor Probus planted this mountain with vines (Eutropius).
