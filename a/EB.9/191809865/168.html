ATTALICÆ VESTES

article · 243 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ATTALICÆ VESTES, in Antiquity, garments made of a kind of cloth of gold. They took the denomination from Attalus, surnamed Philometor, a wealthy king of Pergamus, who was the first, according to Pliny, who procured gold to be woven into cloth.
