ATHLETE, in Antiquity, persons of strength and agility, disciplined to perform in the public games. The word is originally Greek, ἀθλῆς: formed from ἀθλῆ, certain, "combat;" whence also ἀθλῆς, the prize or reward adjudged the victor.—Under athletes were comprehended wrestlers, boxers, runners, leapers, throwers.

throwers of the disk, and those practised in other exercises exhibited in the Olympic, Pythian, and other solemn sports; for the conquerors wherein there were established prizes.
