BANISHMENT

BANISHMENT, exile, among us is of two kinds: the one voluntary, and upon oath; the other by compulsion, for some offence or crime. The former properly called abjuration, is now ceased; the latter is chiefly enjoined by judgment of parliament. Yet outlawing and transportation may also be considered as species of exile.
