CHARPENTIER

CHARPENTIER, FRANCIS, dean of the French academy, was born in 1620. His early capacity inclined his friends to educate him at the bar: but he was much more delighted with the study of languages and antiquity than of the law; and preferred repose to tumult. M. Colbert made use of him in establishing his new academy of medals and inscriptions; and no person of that learned society contributed more than himself toward that noble series of medals which were struck on the considerable events that distinguished the reign of Louis XIV.
