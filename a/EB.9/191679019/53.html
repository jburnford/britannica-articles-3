HYPERIDES

article · 333 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

HYPERIDES, an orator of Greece, was the disciple of Plato and Iſocrates, and governed the republic of Athens. He defended with great zeal and courage the liberties of Greece; but was put to death by Antipater's order, 322 B. C. He composed many orations, of which only one now remains. He was one of the ten celebrated Greek orators.
