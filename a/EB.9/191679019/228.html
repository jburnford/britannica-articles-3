IDALIUM

article · 412 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

IDALUM, in Ancient Geography, a promontory on the east side of Cyprus. Now Capo di Griego; with a high rugged eminence rising over it, in the form of a table. It was sacred to Venus; and hence the epithet Idalia given her by the poets. The eminence was covered by a grove; and in the grove was a little town, in Pliny's time extinct. Idalia, according to Bochart, denotes the place or spot sacred to the goddess.
