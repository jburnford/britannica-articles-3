JAR

article · 311 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

JAR, or JARR, an earthen pot or pitcher, with a big belly and two handles.—The word comes from the Spanish jarra or jarro, which signifies the same.

JAR is used for a sort of measure or fixed quantity of divers things.—The jar of oil is from 18 to 26 gallons; the jar of green ginger is about 100 pounds weight.
