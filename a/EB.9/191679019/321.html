ILCHESTER

article · 1,125 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ILCHESTER, a town of Somersetshire in England, seated on the river Yeovil, 129 miles from London, is so called, because it once had a castle, and stands on the river Ivel. It is a place of great antiquity, as appears by the Roman coins which are sometimes dug up. It is likewise evident, from the ruins and from two towers on the bridge, that it was once a large place, and encompassed with a double wall. It also had several parish-churches, though now but one. It is governed by two bailiffs, who with the twelve burghesses are lords of the manor. In the reign of Edward III. the assizes for the county were fixed here, which have since been held
