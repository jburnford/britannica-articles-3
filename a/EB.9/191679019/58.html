HYPNOTICUS SERPENS

HYPNOTICUS SERPENS, the Sleep-snake, in Zoology, the name of an East Indian species of serpent, called by the Ceylonese nintapalong, a word importing the same sense. It is of a deep blackish brown, variegated with spots of white, and is a very fatal kind in its poison: its bite it is said brings on a sleep which ends in death; hence this trivial name.
