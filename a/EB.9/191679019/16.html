HYDRUNTUM, in Ancient Geography, a noble and commodious port of Calabria, from which there was a shorter passage to Apollonia (Pliny). Famous for its antiquity, and for the fidelity and bravery of its inhabitants. Now Otranto, a city of Naples, at the entrance of the gulf of Venice. E. Long. 19. 15. N. Lat. 40. 12.
