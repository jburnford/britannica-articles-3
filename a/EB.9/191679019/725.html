INTUITION

article · 299 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Cross-references: LOGIC

INTUITION, among logicians, the act whereby the mind perceives the agreement or disagreement of two ideas, immediately by themselves, without the intervention of any other; in which case the mind perceives the truth as the eye does the light, only by being directed towards it. See LOGIC, No 25, 27.
