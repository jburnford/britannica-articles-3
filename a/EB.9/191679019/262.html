JENISKOI

JENISKOI, a town of the Russian empire, in Siberia, seated on the river Jeniska. It is large, populous, and pretty strong; and there are villages for several miles round it. It is subject to the Tungusians, who are Pagans, and live chiefly on the above river. They pay a tribute to the emperor for every how, reckoning a man and a woman for one. The climate is extremely cold; and no other fruits grow there but black and red currants, strawberries, and gooseberries. Corn, butchers meat, and wild fowls, are very cheap. E. Long. 92. 35. N. Lat. 57. 46.
