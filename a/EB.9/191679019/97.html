JABLONSKI

JABLONSKI, Theodore, counsellor of the court of Prussia, and secretary of the royal academy of sciences in Berlin, was also a man of distinguished merit. He loved the sciences, and did them honour, without that ambition which is generally seen in men of learning; it was owing to this modesty that the greatest part of his works were published without his name. He published, in 1711, a French and German Dictionary; a Course of Morality, in 1713; a Dictionary of Arts and Sciences, 1721; and translated Tacitus de moribus Germanorum into High Dutch, in 1724.
