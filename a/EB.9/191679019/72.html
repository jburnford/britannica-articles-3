Hypoglossi of the lower region of the belly. See ANATOMY, No 88.

Hypoglossi. HYPOGLOSSI EXTERNI, or MAJORES, in Anatomy, the ninth pair of nerves, called also linguales and gustatorii. See ANATOMY.

HYPOGLOSSIS or HYPOGLOSSIS, (composed of υπο under, and γλωσσα tongue), in Anatomy, is a name given to two glands of the tongue. There are four large glands of the tongue; two of them called hypoglossides, situated under it, near the venæ ranulares: one on each side of the tongue. They serve to secrete a kind of serous matter of the nature of saliva, which is discharged into the mouth by little ducts near the gums.
