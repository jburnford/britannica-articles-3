HYPOGÆUM

article · 312 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

HYPOGÆUM, ὑπογῶνιον, formed of ὑπὲρ under, and γῆ earth, in the ancient architecture, is a name common to all the parts of a building that are under ground; as the cellar, butteries, and the like places. The term hypogæum was used by the Greeks and Romans for subterraneous tombs in which they buried their dead.
