IRROGATIO

article · 584 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

IRROGATIO, a law term amongst the Romans, signifying the instrument in which were put down the punishments which the law provided against such offences as any person was accused of by a magistrate before the people. These punishments were first proclaimed vox voce by the accuser, and this was called Inquisitio. The same, being immediately after expressed in writing, took the name of Rogatio, in respect of the people, who were to be consulted or asked about it, and was called Irrigatio in respect of the criminal, as it imported the malt or punishment assigned him by the accuser.
