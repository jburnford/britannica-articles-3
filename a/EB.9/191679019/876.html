ISIDORUS

article · 382 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ISIDORUS, called DAMATENSIS, or PELUSIOTA, from his living in a solitude near that city, was one of the most famous of all St Chrysostom's disciples, and flourished in the time of the general council held in 421. We have 2012 of his epistles in five books. They are short, but well written, in Greek. The best edition is that of Paris, in Greek and Latin, printed in 1638, in folio.
