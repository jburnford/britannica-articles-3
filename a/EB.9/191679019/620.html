INOA, festivals in memory of Ino, celebrated yearly with sports and sacrifices at Corinth. An anniversary sacrifice was also offered to Ino at Megara, where she was first worshipped under the name of Leucothoe.—Another in Laconia, in honour of the same. It was usual at the celebration to throw cakes of flour into a pond, which if they sunk were presages of prosperity, but if they swam on the surface of the waters they were inauspicious and very unlucky.
