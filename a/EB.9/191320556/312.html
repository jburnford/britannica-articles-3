CALADE

article · 375 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CALADE, in the manège, the descent or sloping declivity of a rising manège ground, being a small eminence upon which we ride down a horse several times, putting him to a short gallop, with his fore hams in the air, to learn him to ply or bend his haunches, and form his stop upon the aides of the calves of the legs, the stay of the bridle, and the cavesson seasonably given.
