CARRUCA

article · 310 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CARRUCA, in Antiquity, a splendid kind of carr, or chariot, mounted on four wheels, richly decorated with gold, silver, ivory, &c. in which the emperors, senators and people of condition, were carried. The word comes from the Latin carrus, or British carr, which is still the Irish name for any wheel-carriage.
