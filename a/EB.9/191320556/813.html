CAPER

CAPER also denotes a vessel used by the Dutch for cruising and taking prizes from the enemy; in which sense, caper amounts to the same with privateer. Capers are commonly double officered, and crowded with hands even beyond the rates of ships of war, because the thing chiefly in view is boarding the enemies.
