CELTS, certain ancient instruments of a wedge-like form, of which several have been discovered in different parts of Great Britain. Antiquarians have generally attributed them to the Celts; but not agreeing as to their use, distinguished them by the above unmeaning appellation. But Mr Whittaker makes it probable that they were British battle-axes. See BATTLE-AXE.
