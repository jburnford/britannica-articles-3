BUTUS

article · 419 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BUTUS, in Ancient Geography, a town of Lower Egypt, on the west side of the branch of the Nile, called Thermuthiacus; towards the mouth called Ostium Sebenniticum; in this town stood an oracle of Latona, (Strabo, Herodotus). Ptolemy places Butus in the Nomos Phthenotes; it is also called Buto, -us, (Herodotus, Stephanus). It had temples of Apollo and Diana, but the largest was that of Latona, where the oracle stood.
