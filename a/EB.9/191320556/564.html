CAMISADE

article · 324 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CAMISADE, in the art of war, an attack by surprise in the night, or at the break of day, when the enemy is supposed to be a-bed. The word is said to have taken its rise from an attack of this kind; wherein, as a badge or signal to know one another by, they bore a shift, in French called chemise, or camise, over their arms.
