CADARI

article · 413 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CADARI, or KADARI, a sect of Mahometans, who assert free will; attribute the actions of men to merit alone, not to any secret power determining the will; and deny all absolute decrees, and predestination. The author of this sect was Mabeb ben Kaled al Gihoni, who suffered martyrdom for it. The word comes from the Arabic, قدر, cadara, "power." Ben Aun calls the Cadarians the Magi or Manichees of the Musselmans.
