CANNEQUINS

article · 256 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CANNEQUINS, in commerce, white cotton cloths brought from the East Indies. They are a proper commodity for trading on the coast of Guinea, particularly about the rivers Senegal and Gambia. These linens are folded square-wise, and are about eight ells long.
