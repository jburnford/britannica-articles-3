CADENCE

CADENCE, in Reading, is a falling of the voice below the key note at the close of every period. In reading, whether prose or verse, a certain tone is assumed which is called the key note; and in this tone the bulk of the words are sounded: but this note is generally lowered towards the close of every sentence.
