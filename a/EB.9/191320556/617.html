CAMUS, John Peter, a French prelate born in 1582. He was author of a number of pious romances (the talte of his time), and other theological works, to the amount of 200 vols. His definition of politics is remarkable: Ars non tam regendi, quam fallendi, boni-net; "The art not so much of governing, as of deceiving mankind." He died in 1652.
