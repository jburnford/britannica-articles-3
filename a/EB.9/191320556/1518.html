CELETES

article · 330 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CELETES, or CELETA, (from κἄλς, a race-horse), in antiquity, denote single or saddle horses, by way of contradistinction from those yoked or harnessed together, called bigarii, quadrigarii, &c. The same denomination is also given to the cavaliers or riders on horseback; and hence some deduce celeres, the name of Romulus's guard.
