CARUS baronie

sub_entry · 456 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CARUS baronie, the head of the barony, in ancient customs, denotes the ancient or chief seat or castle of a nobleman, where he made his usual residence, and held his court; sometimes also called caput honoris, or the head of the honour. The caput baronie could not be settled in dowry; nor could it be divided among the daughters, in case there was no son to inherit; but was to descend entire to the eldest daughter, ceteris filiabus aliunde satisfacitis.
