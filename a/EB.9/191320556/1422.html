CAVALCADOUR

article · 368 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CAVALCADOUR, or CATALCADEUR, anciently denoted a riding matter; but at present is disused in that sense, and only employed to denote a sort of equestrians or officers who have the direction of princes stables. The French say, ecuyer cavalcadour of the king, the duke of Orleans, &c. Menage writes it cavalcadour, and derives it from the Spanish cavalgador, a horseman.
