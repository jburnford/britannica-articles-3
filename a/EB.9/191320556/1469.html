Antecedent CAUSE

sub_entry · 463 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Antecedent CAUSE, (αἴτια προηγούμενα), a latent disposition of the body, from whence some disease may arise; such as a plethora in respect of a fever, a cacochymia in respect of a scurvy.

Cause
||
Causality. Continent, Conjunct, or Proximate Cause, that principle in the body which immediately adheres to the disease, and which being present, the disease is also present; or, which being removed, the disease is taken away: such is the stone in a nephritic patient.
