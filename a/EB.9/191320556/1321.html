CASUS AMMISSIONIS

CASUS AMMISSIONIS, in Scots Law. In actions proving the tenor of obligations inextinguishable by the debtor's retiring or cancelling them, it is necessary for the pursuer, before he is allowed a proof of the tenor, to condescend upon such a casus amissionis, or accident

by which the writing was destroyed, as shows it was lost while in the writer's possession.
