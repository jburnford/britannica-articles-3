CATAGRAPH

article · 331 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CATAGRAPH, in antiquity, denote oblique figures or views of men's faces; answering to what the moderns call profiles.

Catagrapha are said to be the invention of Simon Cleoneus, who first taught painters to vary the looks of their figures, and sometimes direct them upwards, sometimes downwards, and sometimes sideways or backwards.
