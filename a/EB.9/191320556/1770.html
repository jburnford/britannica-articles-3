CHAMIER, DANIEL, an eminent Protestant divine, born in Dauphiny. He was many years preacher at Montelimart; from whence he went in 1612 to Montaubon, to be professor of divinity in that city, and was killed by a cannon ball during the siege in 1621. The most considerable of his works is his Panfratia Catholica, or, "Wars of the Lord," in four volumes folio; in which he treats very learnedly of the controversies between the Protestants and Roman Catholics.
