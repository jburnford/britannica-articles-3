BUSTUARII

article · 386 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BUSTUARII, in Roman antiquity, gladiators who fought about the bustum or funeral pile of a person of distinction, that the blood which was spilt might serve as a sacrifice to the infernal gods, and render them more propitious to the manes of the deceased. This custom was introduced in the room of the more inhu-

man one of sacrificing captives at the bustum, or on the tombs of warriors.
