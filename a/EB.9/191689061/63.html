PIABUCU

article · 297 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PIABUCU, in Ichthyology, is an American fish, eaten in many places by the natives. It is said to be so ravenous, and greedy of blood, that if a person go into the water with a wound in any part of his body, the piabucu will make up to it to suck the blood. It seldom exceeds four inches in length.
