PIGUS

article · 774 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PIGUS, in Ichthyology, is the name of a species of leather-mouthed fish, very much resembling the nature of the common carp; being of the same shape and size, and its eyes, fins, and fleshy palate, exactly the same; from the gills to the tail there is a crooked dotted line; the back and sides are bluish, and the belly reddish. It is covered with large scales; from the middle of each of which there rises a fine pellucid pricklet, which is very sharp. It is an excellent fish for the table, being perhaps preferable to the carp; and it is in season in the months of March and April. It is caught in lakes in some parts of Italy, and is mentioned by Pliny, though without a name. Arteri says it is a species of cyprinus, and he calls it the cyprinus, called pico and pigus.
