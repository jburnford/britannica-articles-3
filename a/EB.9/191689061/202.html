PINT

Cross-references: MEASURE

PINT (pinta), a vessel, or measure, used in estimating the quantity of liquids, and even sometimes of dry things.—Buccus derives the word from the Greek πίντα; others from the German pint, a little measure of wine; Nicod from the Greek πίνω, "to drink."

The English pint is twofold; the one for wine measure, the other for beer and ale measure. See MEASURE.
