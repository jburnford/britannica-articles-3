PILEWORT

PILEWORT (Ranunculus ficaria, Lin.), the root. This is a very small plant, found in moist meadows and by hedge sides. The roots consist of slender fibres with some little tubercles among them, which are supposed to resemble the haemorrhoids. From thence it has been concluded, that this root must needs be of wonderful efficacy for the cure of that disease: to the taste, it is little other than mucilaginous; and although still retained in several of the foreign pharmacopoeias, it is hardly in use in this country.
