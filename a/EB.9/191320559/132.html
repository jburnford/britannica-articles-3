POMUM, an APPLE; a species of feed-vessel, composed of a succulent fleshy pulp; in the middle of which

is generally found a membranous capsule, with a number of cells, or cavities, for containing the seeds. Seed-vessels of this kind have no external opening or valve. At the end opposite to the footstalk is frequently a small cavity, called by the gardeners the eye of the fruit, and by botanists umbilicus, the "navel," from its fancied resemblance to the navel in animals. Gourd, cucumber, melon, pomegranate, pear, and apple, furnish instances of the fruit or seed-vessel in question.
