TOGA PRETEXTA

sub_entry · 258 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

TOGA PRETEXTA, among the ancient Romans, a long white gown, with a border of purple round the edges, and worn by children of quality till the age of puberty, viz. by the boys till 17, when they changed it for the toga virilis; and by the girls till marriage.
