PREENING

article · 337 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PREENING, in Natural History, the action of birds cleaning, composing, and dressing their feathers, to enable them to glide more easily through the air. For this purpose they have two peculiar glands on their rump, which secrete an unctuous matter into a bag that is perforated, out of which the bird occasionally draws it with its bill.
