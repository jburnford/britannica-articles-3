POX, French-POX, or Lues Venerea. See MEDICINE, No 350.

Small-POX. See INOCULATION, and MEDICINE, No 222—226.

POYNING'S LAW, an act of Parliament made in Ireland under Henry VII. whereby all the statutes of force in England were made of force in Ireland; which before that time they were not.—Nor are any now in force there made in England since that time.

The law took its name from Sir Edward Poyning, lord-lieutenant of that kingdom at the time of its making. See IRELAND, No 46.
