PROBOSCIS

article · 258 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PROBOSCIS, in Natural History, is the trunk or snout of an elephant, and some other animals and insects.

Flies, gnats, &c. are furnished with a proboscis or trunk; by means of which they suck the blood of animals, the juice of vegetables, &c. for their food.
