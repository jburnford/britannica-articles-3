PROBE, a surgeon's instrument for examining the circumstances of wounds, ulcers, and other cavities, searching for stones in the bladder, &c.

PROBITY means honesty, sincerity, or veracity; and consists in the habit of actions useful to society, and in the constant observance of the laws which justice and

and conscience impose on us. The man who obeys all the laws of society with an exact punctuality is not therefore a man of probity; laws can only respect the external and definite parts of human conduct, but probity respects our more private actions, and such as it is impossible in all cases to define; and it appears to be in morals what charity is in religion. Probity teaches us to perform in society those actions which no external power can oblige us to perform, and is that quality in the human mind from which we claim the performance of the rights commonly called imperfect. See MORAL PHILOSOPHY.
