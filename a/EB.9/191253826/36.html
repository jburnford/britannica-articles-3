BRIDGEWATER

article · 385 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BRIDGEWATER, a town of Somersetshire in England, seated on the river Parret, over which there is a stone-bridge, near which ships of 100 tons burden may ride with ease. It is a large well frequented place, with the title of a duchy, and sends two members to parliament. There are in it several large inns, and the market is well supplied with provisions. W. Long. 3. 0. N. Lat. 51. 15.
