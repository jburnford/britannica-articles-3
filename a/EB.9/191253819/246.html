ALCMANIAN

article · 254 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ALCMANIAN, in Ancient Lyric Poetry, a kind of verse consisting of two dactyles and two trochees; as,—

Virginibus puerisque cantis.

The word is formed from Alcman, the name of an ancient Greek poet, in great esteem for his erotics or amorous compositions.
