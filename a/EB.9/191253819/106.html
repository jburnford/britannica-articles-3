AKISSAT

AKISSAT, the ancient Thyatira, a city in Natolia, in Asia, situated in a plain 18 miles broad, which produces plenty of cotton and grain. The inhabitants who are reckoned to be about 5000, are said to be all Mahometans. The houses are built of nothing but earth or turf dried in the sun, and are very low and ill contrived: but there are six or seven mosques, which are all of marble. There are remarkable inscriptions on marble in several parts of the town, which are part of the ruins of the ancient Thyatira. It is seated on the river Hermus, 50 miles from Pergamos. E. Long. 28. 30. N. Lat. 38. 50.
