ALMADIE

article · 322 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ALMADIE, a kind of canoe, or small vessel, about four fathoms long, commonly made of bark, and used by the negroes of Africa.

ALMADIE is also the name of a kind of long boats, fitted out at Calicut, which are eighty feet in length, and six or seven in breadth. They are exceedingly swift, and are otherwise called cahurri.
