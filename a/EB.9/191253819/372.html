ALIPILARIUS

article · 273 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ALIPILARIUS, or ALIPILUS, in Roman Antiquity, a servant belonging to the baths, whose business it was, by means of waxen plasters, and an instrument called voisella, to take off the hairs from the arm pits, and even arms, legs, &c. this being deemed a point of cleanliness.
