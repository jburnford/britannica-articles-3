NEOPHYTES

article · 404 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

NEOPHYTES, "new plants;" a name given by the ancient Christians to those heathens who had newly embraced the faith; such persons being considered as regenerated, or born anew by baptism. The term neophytes has been also used for new priests, or those just admitted into orders, and sometimes for the novices in monasteries. It is still applied to the converts made by the missionaries among the infidels.
