NABLUM

article · 257 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

NABLUM, in Hebrew, Nebel, was an instrument of music among the Jews. It had strings like the harp, and was played upon by both hands. Its form was that of a Greek A. In the Septuagint and Vulgate, it is called noblum, psalterion, lyra; and sometimes ethara.
