MOULDINGS

MOULDINGS, in Architecture, projections beyond the naked wall, column, wainscot, &c. the assemblage of which forms cornices, door cases, and other decorations of ARCHITECTURE. See that article.

MOULINET is used, in Mechanics, to signify a roller, which, being crossed with two levers, is usually applied to cranes, capstans, and other sorts of engines of the like nature, to draw ropes, heave up stones, &c.

MOULINET is also a kind of turnstile, or wooden cross, which turns horizontally upon a stake fixed in the ground; usually placed in passages to keep out horses, and to oblige passengers to go and come one by one. These moulinets are often set near the outworks of

Meals of fortified places at the sides of the barriers, through which people pass on foot.
