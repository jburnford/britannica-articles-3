MYGDONIA

article · 390 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MYGDONIA, in Ancient Geography, a district of
Macedonia, to the north of the Sinus Thermaicus, and
east of the river Axius, which separates it from Bo-
tæis, and west of the river Strymon, (Pliny). Also a
district of Mesopotamia, which took its name from that
of Macedonia, running along the Euphrates, from
Zeugma down to Thapsacus, extending a great way
east, because Nisibis was reckoned to it.
