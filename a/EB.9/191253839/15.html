MOREA

Cross-references: GREECE

MOREA, formerly called the Peloponnesus, is a peninsula to the south of Greece, to which it is joined by the isthmus of Corinth. Its form resembles a mulberry leaf, and its name is derived from the great number of mulberry trees which it produces. It is about 180 miles in length, and 130 in breadth. The air is temperate, and the land fertile, except in the middle, where it is full of mountains, and is watered by a great number of rivers. It is divided into three provinces; Scania, Belvedera, and Bizazzo-di-Maini. It was taken from the Turks by the Venetians in 1687; but they lost it again in 1715. The fangiac of the Morea resides at Modon. See GREECE and PELOPONNESUS.
