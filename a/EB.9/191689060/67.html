PATAVIUM

article · 357 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PATAVIUM (Tacitus, Strabo), a town of the Transpadana, situated on the left or north bank of the Medocus Minor; founded by Antenor the Trojan, (Mela, Virgil, Seneca); Patavini, the people, (Livy); who himself was a native, and by Aſinius Pollio charged with patavinity. Now Padua, in the territory and to the west of Venice. E. Long. 12. 15. N. Lat. 45. 30.
