PELTA

article · 266 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PELTA, a small, light, manageable buckler, used by the ancients. It was worn by the Amazons. The pelta is said by some to have resembled an ivy leaf in form; by others it is compared to the leaf of an Indian fig tree; and by Serbius to the moon in her first quarter.
