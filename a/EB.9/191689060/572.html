PHÆA

article · 365 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PHÆA, in Antiquity, a famous fow which infested the neighbourhood of Croyon. Theseus destroyed it as he was travelling from Trozene to Athens to make himself known to his father. Some imagine that the boar of Calydon sprang from this fow. According to some authors, Phæa was a woman who prostituted herself to strangers, whom she murdered, and afterwards plundered.
