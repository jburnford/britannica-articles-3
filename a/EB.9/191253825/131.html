BOVEY COAL

article · 277 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BOVEY COAL, an inflammable fossil found in England, France, Italy, Switzerland, Germany, Ireland, &c. Its colour is brown or brownish black, and of a laminar structure. It is composed of wood, penetrated by petrol or bitumen; and frequently contains pyrites, alum, and vitriol.
