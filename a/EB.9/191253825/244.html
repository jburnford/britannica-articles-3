BRACES

article · 763 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BRACES, in the sea-language, are ropes belonging to all the yards of a ship, except the mizen, two to each yard, reeved through blocks that are fastened to pennants, seized to the yard-arms. Their use is either to square or traverse the yards. Hence to brace the yard, is to bring it to either side. All braces come aftward on; as, the main brace comes to the poop, the main-top-sail brace comes to the mizen-top and thence to the main-shrouds; the fore and fore-top-sail braces come down by the main and main-top-sail stays, and so of the rest. But the mizen-bowline serves to brace to the yard, and the cross-jack braces are brought forwards to the main-shrouds, when the ship fails close by a wind.

BRACES of a Coach, thick straps of leather on which it hangs.
