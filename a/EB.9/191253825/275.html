BRADY, ROBERT

article · 460 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BRADY, ROBERT, born in Norfolk in 1643, was master of Caius college, Cambridge, regius professor there, and twice representative of that university in parliament. In 1685, he was made keeper of the records in the tower, and was physician in ordinary to James II. He wrote, An introduction to the Old English history; A history of England, from the time of the Romans to the end of the reign of Richard II.; and, A treatise on English boroughs. He died in 1700.
