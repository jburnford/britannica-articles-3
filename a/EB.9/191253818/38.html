ABALUS

article · 260 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ABALUS, in Ancient Geography, supposed by the ancients to be an island in the German ocean, called by Timæus Bastia, and by Xenophon Lampacenus Baltia; now the peninsula of Scandinavia. Here, according to Pliny, some imagined that amber dropped from the trees.
