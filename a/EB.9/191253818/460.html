ACELDAMA

article · 374 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ACELIDAMA, in Scripture History, a place without the south wall of Jerusalem, beyond the brook of Siloam, was called the Potters field, because clay of which pots were made was dug out of it. It was afterwards bought with the money with which the high priests and rulers of the Jews purchased the blood of Jesus Christ, and hence it was called Acelidama, the field of blood.
