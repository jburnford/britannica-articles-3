ABORAS

article · 277 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ABORAS, in Ancient Geography, by Xenophon called Araxes, a river of Mesopotamia, which flows into the Euphrates at Cercefium. In the negotiation between Diocletian and Narses, near the end of the third century, it was fixed as the boundary between the Roman and Persian empire.
