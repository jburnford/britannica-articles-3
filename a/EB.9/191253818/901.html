ADRIAN V

ADRIAN V. Pope, a Genoeſe, whose name was Otoboni Fieschi, succeeded Innocent V. A. D. 1276. He was by his uncle Innocent IV. created cardinal deacon of St Adrian, and in 1254 sent by him to England, to settle the disputes between Henry III. and his barons. He was employed again for the same purpose, by Clement III. when he issued a sentence of excommunication against the king's enemies. When he was congratulated on his accession to the papal chair, he said, "I with you had found me a healthy cardinal, rather than a dying pope." After his election he went to Viterbo to meet the emperor Rodolphus, for

the purpose of opposing the usurpation of Charles, king of the Two Sicilies; but died soon after his arrival, having enjoyed his dignity only thirty-eight days. He zealously encouraged the crusade to the Holy Land, and upon his election sent a large sum to Constantinople towards building galleys.
