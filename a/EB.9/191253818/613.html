ACRA, in Ancient Geography, one of the hills of Jerusalem, on which stood the lower town, which was the old Jerusalem; to which was afterwards added Zion, or the city of David. Probably called Acra, from the fortress which Antiochus built there in order to annoy the temple, and which Simon Maccabæus took and razed to the ground.
