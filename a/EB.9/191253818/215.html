ABOLA

article · 365 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ABOLA, in Geography, a division of the Agow, in Abyssinia, is a narrow valley, through which runs a river of the same name, whose waters receive many tributary streams from the lofty, rugged, and woody mountains that form the valley. In none of the rivers are any fish found, which Bruce ascribes to their being dried up in the summer, and great rapidity in winter.
