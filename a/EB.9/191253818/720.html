ACUTIATOR

article · 291 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ACUTIATOR, in writers of the barbarous ages, denotes a person that whets or grinds cutting instruments; called also in ancient glossaries acutor, acutator, famarius, cobarius, &c. In the ancient armies there were acutitores, a kind of smiths, retained for whetting or keeping the arms sharp.
