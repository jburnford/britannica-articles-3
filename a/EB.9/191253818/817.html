ADJUDICATION

article · 347 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ADJUDICATION, in Scots Law, the name of that action by which a creditor attaches the heritable estate of his debtor, or his debtor's heir, in order to appropriate it to himself, either in payment or security of his debt; or that action by which the holder of an heritable right, labouring under any defect in point of form, may supply that defect.
