ÆGEAN SEA

ÆGEAN SEA, in Ancient Geography, now the Archipelago, a part of the Mediterranean, separating Europe from Asia; washing, on the one hand, Greece and Macedonia; on the other, Caria and Ionia. The origin of the name is greatly disputed. Pausanias advances three opinions: one, that it is so called from the many islands therein, at a distance appearing like so many goats; another, because Ægea queen of the Amazons perished in it: a third opinion is, because Ægeus, the father of Theseus, threw himself headlong into it.
