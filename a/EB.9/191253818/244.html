ABRAVANNUS

article · 220 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ABRAVANNUS, in Ancient Geography, the name of a promontory and river of Galloway in Scotland, so called from the Celtic term Aber, signifying either the mouth of a river or the confluence of two rivers, and Aon, a river.
