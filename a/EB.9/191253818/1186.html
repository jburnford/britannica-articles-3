AGGREGATE

Cross-references: BOTANY

AGGREGATE, in Botany, is a term used to express those flowers which are composed of parts or florets, so united, by means either of the receptacle or calyx,

N n

that no one of them can be taken away without destroying the form of the whole. They are opposed to simple flowers, which have no such common part. See BOTANY Index.
