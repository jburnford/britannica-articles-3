ACROBATES

article · 282 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ACROBATES, in Antiquity, were rope-dancers
who performed various feats by vaulting or tumbling
on a rope; sliding down on a rope from a lofty station

with arms and legs extended, in imitation of flying; Acrobatica
and running, dancing, and leaping, on a rope stretched
horizontally.
