MEAUX

article · 345 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MEAUX, an ancient town of France, in the department of the Seine and Marne, with a bishop's see, seated in a place abounding in corn and cattle, on the river Marne, which divides it into two parts; and its trade consists in corn, wool, and cheese. It sustained a siege of three months against the English in 1421. E. Long. 2. 58. N. Lat. 48. 58.
