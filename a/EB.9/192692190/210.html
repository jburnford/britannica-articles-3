ELAPHEBOLIUM

article · 304 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ELAPHEBOLIUM, in Grecian antiquity, the ninth month of the Athenian year, answering to the latter part of February and beginning of March. It consisted of 30 days; and took its name from the festival elaphebolia, kept in this month, in honour of Diana the huntress, as mentioned in the preceding article.
