ECATESIA

article · 503 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ECATESIA, Εκάτεσις, in antiquity, an anniversary solemnity, observed by the Stratonicenians, in honour of Hecate. The Athenians likewise had a public entertainment or supper every new moon, in honour of the same goddess. The supper was provided at the charge of the richer sort; and was no sooner brought to the accustomed place but the poor people carried all off, giving out that Hecate had devoured it. For the rest of the ceremonies observed on this occasion, see Pott. Arch. Grav. lib. ii. cap. 20.
