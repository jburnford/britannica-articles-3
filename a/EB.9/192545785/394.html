LUGDUNUM

LUGDUNUM, in Ancient Geography, the capital of the Saggiuni in Gallia Celtica, situated at the conflux of the Arar and Rhodanus, on an eminence, as the Celtic term dune signifies; built by Manutius Planicus under Augustus, while commanding in that part of Gaul; and whether he led a colony. Now Lyons, capital of the Lyonnais.
