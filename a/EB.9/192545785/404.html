LULLI, JOHN BAPTIST

article · 702 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

LULLI, JOHN BAPTIST, the most celebrated and most excellent musician that has appeared in France since the revival of learning, was born at Florence. He was taken to France when very young by a person of quality; and he carried the art of playing on the violin to the highest perfection. Louis XIV. made him superintendent of music. Some time after Perrin having introduced operas into France, and quarrelling with his company, he resigned his privilege to Lulli. Operas were then carried to the utmost perfection by this celebrated musician, and were attended with continual

nual applause. Lully every year, after this time, gave a piece of his own composition, till his death, which happened in 1687.
