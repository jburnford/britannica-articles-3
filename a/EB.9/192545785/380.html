LUCUS, in general, denotes a wood or grove fa-

cred to a deity; so called à lucenda, because a great number of lights were usually burning in honour of the god (Isidorus); a practice common with idolaters, as we learn from Scripture: hence Homer's αὐλὸν ἄλυσος.
