LITHOSTROTON

article · 395 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

LITHOSTROTON, among the Romans, was a pavement of mosaic work, consisting of small pieces of cut marble of different kinds and colours, first used in the time of Sylla, who made one at Præneste in the temple of Fortune, and afterwards in private houses; and were brought to such perfection, that they exhibited most lively representations of nature, with all the accuracy of the finest painting.
