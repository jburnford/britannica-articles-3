LYCÆUM

article · 590 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

LYCÆUM, ΛΥΚΕΙΟΝ, in antiquity, the name of a celebrated school or academy at Athens, where Aristotle explained his philosophy. The place was composed of porticoes and trees planted in the quincunx form, where the philosophers disputed walking. Hence philosophy of the Lyceum is used to signify the philosophy of Aristotle, or the Peripatetic philosophy. Saïdas observes, that the Lyceum took its name from its having been originally a temple of Apollo Lyceus; or rather a portico or gallery built by Lyceus son of Apollo; but others mention it to have been built by Pisistratus or Pericles.
