Fossile LINEN

Cross-references: AMIANTHUS

Fossile LINEN, is a kind of amianthus, which consists of flexible, parallel, soft fibres, and which has been celebrated for the uses to which it has been applied, of being woven, and forming an incombustible cloth. Paper also, and wicks for lamps, have been made of it. See AMIANTHUS, ASBESTOS, and MINERALOGY Index.
