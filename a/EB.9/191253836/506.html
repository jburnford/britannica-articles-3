OLIVE Press. In order to obtain the olive oil, the olives are first bruised in a rough trough, under a mill-stone, rolling perpendicularly over them; and when sufficiently mashed, put into the maye or trough, m, of an olive press, where aa are the upright beams, or cheeks; b, the female, and c, the male screw; f, the board on which the screw presses; g, a cubical piece of wood, called a block; h, the peel, a circular board, to be put under the block. By turning the screw, all the liquor.
