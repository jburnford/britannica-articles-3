OPTATIVE MOOD

article · 292 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

OPTATIVE MOOD, in Grammar, that which serves to express an ardent desire or wish for something.

In most languages, except the Greek, the optative is only expressed by prefixing to the subjunctive an adverb of wishing: as utinam, in Latin; plut à Dieu, in French; and would to God, in English.
