OARISTHUS

article · 426 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

OARISTHUS, or OARISTYS, a term in the Greek poetry, signifying a dialogue between a husband and his wife; such as that in the sixth book of the Iliad between Hector and Andromache.

Scaliger observes, that the oaristhus is not properly any particular little poem, or entire piece of poetry; but always a part of a great one. He adds, that the passage now cited in Homer is the only proper oaristhus extant in the ancient poets.
