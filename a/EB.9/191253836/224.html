NOVARA, an ancient and strong city of Italy, in the duchy of Milan, and capital of the Novarese. Some pretend that this city was built by the Trojans, and so called quasi Nova Ara, because they had erected there a temple to Venus. Tacitus mentions its being made a municipal city by the Romans, and there are many inscriptions still extant, which sufficiently prove its ancient splendour. It is now a small but well-built town, situated on a little eminence, in a fine country, betwixt two rivers, very well fortified, and is the see of a bishop suffragan of Milan. It is remarkable for the several sieges sustained in past times, and for being the birth-place of Peter Lombard, master of the sentences. E. Long. 8. 35. N. Lat. 45. 25.
