HELIADES

article · 267 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

HELIADES, in Mythology, the daughters of the Sun and Clymene, according to the poets. They were so afflicted, as they say, with the death of their brother Phaeton, that the gods, moved with compassion, transformed them into poplars on the banks of the river Eridanus.
