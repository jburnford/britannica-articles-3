GRANI

article · 326 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

GRANI, in our ancient writers, mustachoes or whiskers of a beard. The word seems formed from the ancient British or Irish greann, "a beard." It is given for a reason why the cup is refused to the laity, Quia barbati, & prolixos habent granos, dum poculum inter epulas sumunt, prius liquore pilos inficiunt, quam ori infundunt.
