HAILING

article · 262 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

HAILING, the salutation or accosting of a ship at a distance, either at sea, or in a harbour. The usual expression is, "Hooa, the ship ahoay!" To which the answers, "Holloa? Whence came ye? Where are ye bound? Good voyage! What cheer? All well! How fare ye?" &c.
