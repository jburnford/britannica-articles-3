HATCHING

article · 533 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

HATCHING, or HACHING, in designing, &c. the making of lines with a pen, pencil, graver, or the like; and the intersecting or going across those lines with others drawn a contrary way, is called counter-hatching. The depths and shadows of draughts are usually formed by hatching.

Hatching is of singular use in heraldry, to distinguish the several colours of a shield, without being illuminated: thus, gules or red is hatched by lines drawn from the top to the bottom; azure, by lines drawn across the shield; and so of other colours.
