GYMNOSPERMIA

Cross-references: BOTANY

GYMNOSPERMIA, in Botany, (from γυμνός "naked," and σπερμα "seed;") the first order in Linnaeus's class of didynamina. It comprehends the plants of that class which have naked seeds. The seeds are constantly four in number, except in one genus, viz. phryma, which is monospermous. See BOTANY, p. 65, and 211.
