HALYMOTE, properly signifies a holy or ecclesiastical court. See HALMOTE.

There is a court held in London by this name before the lord mayor and sheriffs, for regulating the bankers. It was anciently held on Sunday next before St Thomas's day, and for this reason called the Halymote, or Holy-court.
