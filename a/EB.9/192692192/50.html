GRAMPIAN HILLS; a chain of high mountains in Scotland, which run from east to west almost the whole breadth of the kingdom. See (Scottish) ALPS and SCOTLAND.—They take their name from only a single hill, the Mons Grampius of Tacitus, where Galgacus waited the approach of Agricola, and where the battle was fought so fatal to the brave Caledonians.
