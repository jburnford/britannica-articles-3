HÆMUS

article · 238 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

HÆMUS, in Ancient Geography, a vast ridge, running from Illyricum toward the Euxine, (Pliny); so high as to afford a prospect both of the Euxine and Adriatic. Here, in after ages, was constituted a province called Hæmimous, or Hæmimontus.
