GROOM, a name particularly applied to several superior officers belonging to the king's household, as groom of the chamber, groom of the stole. See STOLE, and WARDROBE.

GROOM is more particularly used for a servant appointed to attend on horses in the stable.—The word is formed from the Flemish grom, "a boy."
