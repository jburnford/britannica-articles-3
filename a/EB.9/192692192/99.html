GRATINGS

article · 281 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

GRATINGS, in a ship, are small edges of sawed plank, framed one into another like a lattice or prison grate, lying on the upper deck, between the mainmast and foremast, serving for a defence in a close fight, and also for the coolness, light, and conveniency of the ship's company.
