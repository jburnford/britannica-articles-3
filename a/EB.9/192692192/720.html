HAWSE-Pieces

sub_entry · 347 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

HAWSE-Pieces, a name given to the foremost timbers of a ship, whose lower ends rest on the knuckle-timber, or the foremost of the cant-timbers. They are generally parallel to the stem, having their upper ends sometimes terminated by the lower part of the beak-head; and otherwise by the top of the bow, particularly in small ships and merchantmen.
