DUCAI, in general, something belonging to a duke. See DUKE.

The letters patent granted by the senate of Venice are called ducal: so also are the letters wrote, in the name of the senate, to foreign princes. The denomi-

VOL. VII. Part I.

nation of ducal is derived hence; that, at the beginning of such patents, the name of the duke or doge is wrote in capitals, thus, N—Dei Gratia Dux Venetiorum, &c. The date of ducals is usually in Latin, but the body is in Italian. A courier was despatched with a ducal to the emperor, returning him thanks for renewing the treaty of alliance in 1716, against the Turks, with the republic of Venice.
