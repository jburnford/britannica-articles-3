DINDYMA, οἰνυς, (Virgil.) from Dindymus, -is a mountain allotted by many to Phrygia. Strabo has two mountains of this name; one in Myfia near Cyzicus; the other in Gallogrecia near Pessinus; and none in Phrygia. Ptolemy extends this ridge from the borders of Troas, through Phrygia, to Gallogrecia: though therefore there were two mountains called Dindymus in particular, both sacred to the mother of the gods, and none of them in Phrygia Major; yet there
