CURVATURE OF A LINE

article · 402 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CURVATURE OF A LINE, is the peculiar manner of its bending or flexure, by which it becomes a curve of any form and properties. Thus the nature of the curvature of a circle is such, as that every point in the periphery is equally distant from a point within, called the centre; and so the curvature of the same circle is everywhere the same. But the curvature in all other curves is continually varying.
