DRACUNCULI

DRACUNCULI, in Medicine, small long worms which breed in the muscular parts of the arms and legs, called Guinea worms. The common way of getting out these worms is by the point of a needle; and to prevent their forming there again, the usual custom is to wash the parts with wine or vinegar, with alum, nitre, or common salt, or with a strong lixivium of oak ashes, and afterwards anointing them with an ointment of the common kind used for scrofulous eruptions, with a small mixture of quicksilver.
