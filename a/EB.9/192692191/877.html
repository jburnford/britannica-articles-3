DIARTHROSIS

article · 319 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Cross-references: ANATOMY

DIARTHROSIS, in Anatomy, a kind of articulation or juncture of the bones; which being pretty lax affords room for a manifest motion. The word comes from dia, and arthro, juncture, assemblage. It is opposed to synarthrosis, wherein the articulation is so close that there is no sensible motion at all. See ANATOMY, N° 2.
