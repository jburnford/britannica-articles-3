DREUX

article · 529 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DREUX, a town in the Isle of France, remarkable for its antiquities; and for the battle which was fought in December 1562 between the Papists and the Protestants, in which the latter were defeated. Some think it took its name from the priests of Gaul, called the Draids, in the times of Paganism. It consists of two parishes, St Stephen's and Notre Dame, called the great church, which is pretty well built. It is seated on the river Blaise, at the foot of a mountain, on which is a ruined castle. E. Long. 1. 27. N. Lat. 48. 44.
