DOWNS

article · 360 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DOWNS are particularly used for a famous road for ships, along the eastern coast of the county of Kent, from Dover to the North Foreland; where both the outward and homeward bound ships frequently make some stay; and squadrons of men of war rendezvous in time of war.

It affords excellent anchorage; and is defended by the castles of Deal, Dover, and Sandwich.
