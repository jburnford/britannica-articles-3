DIMACHE

article · 282 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DIMACHE, (from dis, double, and μάχη, I fight), in antiquity, a kind of horsemen first instituted by Alexander. Their armour was lighter than that of the infantry, and at the same time heavier than that used by horsemen, so that they could act as horse or foot as occasion required.
