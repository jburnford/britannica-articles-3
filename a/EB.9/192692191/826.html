DIERESIS

article · 349 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DIERESIS, in Medicine, is the confining of the vessels of an animal body, when, from some corroding cause, certain passages are made which naturally ought not

Diuresis to have been; or certain natural passages are dilated beyond their ordinary dimensions, so that the humours which ought to have been contained in the vessels extravasate or run out.
