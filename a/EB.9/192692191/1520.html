Dova-Bird. See UROPA, ORNITHOLOGY Index.

Dova-Meers, in Husbandry, places where soils and dung are mixed and digested together. These consist of pits, prepared at the bottom with stone and clay, that they may hold water, or the moisture of the dung; and ought to be so situated, that the finks and drips of the houses and barns may run into them. Into these pits they cast refuse, fodder, litter, dung, weeds, &c. where they lie and rot together, till the farmer have occasion for them.

Dova-Worms, a species of fly worms, of a short and somewhat flat body, found in great plenty among cow-dung in the months of September and October.
