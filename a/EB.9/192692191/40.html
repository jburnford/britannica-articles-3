CULDEE

article · 377 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CULDEE, in church-history, a sort of monkish priests formerly inhabiting Scotland and Ireland. Being remarkable for the religious exercises of preaching and praying, they were called, by way of eminence, cultores Dei; from whence is derived the word culdee. They made choice of one of their own fraternity to be their spiritual head, who was afterwards called the Scots bishop.
