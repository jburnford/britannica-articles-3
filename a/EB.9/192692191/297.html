DAGON

DAGON, the false god of Ashdod*, or as the Greeks call it Azotus. He is commonly represented as a monster, half man and half fish; whence most learned men derive his name from the Hebrew dag, which signifies "fish." Those who make him to have been the inventor of bread-corn, derive his name from the Hebrew Dagon, which signifies frumentum; whence Philo Biblius calls him Zus Agelios, Jupiter Aratrius.

This deity continued to have a temple at Ashdod

during all the ages of idolatry to the time of the Maccabees; for the author of the first book of Maccabees tells us, that "Jonathan, one of the Maccabees, having beaten the army of Apollonius, Demetrius the general, they fled to Azotus, and entered into Bethdagon (the temple of their idol): but that Jonathan set fire to Azotus, and burnt the temple of Dagon and all those who had fled into it.

Dagon, according to some, was the same with Jupiter, according to others Saturn, according to others, Venus, and according to most, Neptune.
