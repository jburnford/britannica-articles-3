DECAGYNIA

article · 387 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DECAGYNIA (from deka, ten, and gynē, a woman), the name of an order, or secondary division, in the class decandria, of the sexual method, consisting of plants whose flowers are furnished with ten stamens and the same number of styles; which last are considered by Linnaeus and the sexualists as the female organs of generation in plants. Neurada and American nightshade furnish examples.
