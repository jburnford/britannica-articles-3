DEVONSHERING

article · 295 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DEVONSHERING, a term used by the farmers to express the burning of land by way of manure: the method is to cut off the turf about four inches thick, and burn it in heaps, and then spread the ashes upon the land. The name is probably derived from its having been earliest practiced in Devonshire.
