DEFECTIVE

Cross-references: NOUN · VERB

DEFECTIVE, in general, an appellation given to things which want some of the properties that naturally they ought to have. Thus,

DEFECTIVE or Deficient Noun, in Grammar, are such as want either a whole number, a particular case, or are totally indeclinable. See NOUN.

The term defective is also applied to a verb that has not all its moods and tenses. See VERB, MOOD, &c.
