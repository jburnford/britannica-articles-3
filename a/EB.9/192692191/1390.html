DRAIN

article · 321 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DRAIN, a cut, or ditch for carrying off water from the soil, to improve it for the purposes of agriculture. In the ten countries of England drains are 20, 30, and sometimes 40 feet wide, carried through the marshy ground to some river or other place capable of discharging the water which they carry out of the fen lands.
