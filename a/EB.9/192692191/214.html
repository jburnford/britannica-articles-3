CYNÆGIRUS

article · 410 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CYNÆGIRUS, an Athenian, celebrated for his extraordinary courage. He was brother to the poet Æschylus. After the battle of Marathon he pursued the flying Persians to their ships, and seized one of their vessels with his right hand, which was immediately severed by the enemy. Upon this he seized the

Cynanche the vessel with his left hand, and when he had lost that also, he still kept his hold with his teeth.
