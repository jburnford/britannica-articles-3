CYMENE

CYMENE, in Botany, a name given by the ancient Greeks to a plant with which they used to dye woollen stuffs yellow, and with which the women of those times used also to tinge the hair yellow, which was then the favourite colour. The cymene of the Greeks is evidently the same plant with the lutea herba of the Latins: or what is now called dyers weed. See RESEDA, BOTANY INDEX.
