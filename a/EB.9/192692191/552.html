DEFLECTION

article · 374 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DEFLECTION, the turning any thing aside from its former course by some adventitious or external cause. The word is often applied to the tendency of a ship from her true course by reason of currents, &c. which turn her out of her right way. It is likewise applied by astronomers to the tendency of the planets from the line of their projection, or the tangent of their orbit.
