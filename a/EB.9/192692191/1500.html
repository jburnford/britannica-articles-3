DULL

article · 375 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DULL, in the manège. The marks of a dull horse, called by the French marques de ladre, are white spots round the eye and on the tip of the nose, upon any general colour whatsoever. Though the vulgar take these spots for signs of stupidity, it is certain they are great marks of the goodness of a horse; and the horses that have them are very sensible and quick upon the spur.
