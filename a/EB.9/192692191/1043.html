DIPSAS

article · 211 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DIPSAS, a sort of serpent, the bite of which produces such a thirst as proves mortal: whence its name dipsas, which signifies thirsty. In Latin it is called stula, "a pail." Moses speaks of it in Deut. viii. 15.
