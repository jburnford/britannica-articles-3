CURSOR

article · 349 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CURSOR, a clerk belonging to the court of chancery, whose business it is to make out original writs. In the statute 18 Edw. III. they are called clerks of course, and are 24 in number, making a corporation of themselves. To each of them is allowed a division of certain counties, into which they issue out the original writs required by the subject.
