DECIDUOUS

article · 400 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

Cross-references: DEFOLIATION

DECIDUOUS, an appellation chiefly used in respect of plants: thus, the calyx or cup of a flower is said to be deciduous, when it falls along with the flower-petals; and, on the contrary, it is called permanent, when it remains after they are fallen. Again, deciduous leaves are those which fall in autumn; in contradistinction to those of the evergreens, which remain all the winter. See DEFOLIATION.
