DUNSTABLE

article · 528 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DUNSTABLE, a town in Bedfordshire, with a market on Wednesdays. It is seated on a chalky hill; and has ponds in the streets, which are never dry though only supplied with rain-water. It is remarkable for several good inns, it being a great thoroughfare on the northern

northern road. It consists of four streets, intersecting each other at right angles; and in the centre stood one of those beautiful crosses of Queen Eleanor, which was destroyed by the enthusiasts in the time of the civil wars. W. Long. o. 29. N. Lat. 51. 50.
