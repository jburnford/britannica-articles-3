CUTTING

article · 304 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CUTTING, in the manège, is when the horse's feet interfere; or when with the shoe of one foot he beats off the skin from the pastern joint of another foot. This is more frequent in the hind feet than the fore: the causes are either weariness, weakness in the reins, not knowing how to go, or ill shoeing.
