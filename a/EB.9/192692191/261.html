CYZICENS

article · 307 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CYZICENS, CYZICENA, among the ancient Greeks, were a sort of magnificent banqueting houses, always looking toward the north, and usually opening upon gardens.

They had their name from Cyzicus, a city very considerable for the grandeur of its buildings; situated in an island of Myfia, bearing the same name.
