DISSIPATION

article · 303 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DISSIPATION, in Physics, an insensible loss or consumption of the minute parts of the body; or that flux whereby they fly off, and are lost.

Circle of Dissipation, in Optics, is used for that circular space upon the retina, which is taken up by one of the extreme pencils of rays issuing from an object.
