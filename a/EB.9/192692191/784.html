DETTINGEN

article · 346 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

DETTINGEN, a village of Germany, in the circle of the Upper Rhine, and in the territory of Hanau. Here the Austrians and the British, in June 1743, were attacked by the French, who met with a repulse; but as the allies were inferior in number, they could not make the advantage of it they might otherwise have done. E. Long. 8. 45. N. Lat. 50. 8.
