DAMIANISTS

DAMIANISTS, in church-history, a branch of the ancient acephali heresy. They agreed with the catholics in admitting the sixth council, but disowned any distinction of persons in the Godhead; and professed one single nature, incapable of any difference: yet they called God "the Father, Son, and Holy Ghost."
