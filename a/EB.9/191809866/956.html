BINDING-JOISTS

article · 205 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BINDING-JOISTS, in Architecture, are those joists in a floor, into which the trimmers of staircases, or well-holes of the stairs, and chimney-ways, are framed: they ought to be stronger than common joists.
