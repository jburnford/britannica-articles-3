BIDENTES

article · 397 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BIDENTES, in middle-age writers, denotes two yearlings, or sheep of the second year. The wool of these bidentes, or two years old sheep, being the first sheering, was sometimes claimed as a heriot to the king, on the death of an abbot. Among the ancient Romans, the word was extended further to any sorts of beasts used for victims, especially those of that age: whence we meet with suas bidentes.
