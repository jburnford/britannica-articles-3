BLACK-Wodd

Cross-references: MANGANESE

BLACK-Wodd, in Mineralogy, a kind of ore of manganese, remarkable for its property of taking fire when mixed with linseed oil in a certain proportion. It is found in Derbyshire, and is used as a drying ingredient in paints; for when ground with a large quantity of oily matter it loses the property above mentioned. See MANGANESE, MINERALOGY Index.
