BASTARDY

article · 374 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BASTARDY, in relation to its trial in law, is distinguished into general and special. General bastardy is a certificate from the bishop of the diocese, to the king's justices, after inquiry made, whether the party is a bastard or not, upon some question of inheritance. Bastardy special is a suit commenced in the king's courts against a person that calls another a bastard.
