BARKING, a town of Essex in England, seated on the river Roding, not far from the Thames, in a very unwholesome air. It has been chiefly noted for a large monastery, now in ruins; there being nothing left standing but a small part of the walls and a gate-house. E. Long. 0. 13. N. Lat. 51. 30.
