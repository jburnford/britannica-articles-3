BASSETING

article · 334 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BASSETING, in the coal mines, denotes the rise of the vein of coal towards the surface of the earth, till it come within two or three feet of the surface itself. This is also called by the workmen copping; and stands opposed to dipping, which is the descent of the vein to such a depth that it is rarely, if ever, followed to the end.
