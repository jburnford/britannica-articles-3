BISOMUM

BISOMUM, or DISOMUM, in Roman antiquity, a tomb for two dead bodies, or the ashes of two. The ancients frequently buried two, three, or four bodies in the same sepulchre, disposed aside of each other: for it was held an impiety to lay one a-top of another. Hence the sepulchres of the primitive Christians had the words bisomi, trisomi, quadrifomi, &c. inscribed on them to indicate the number of bodies deposited in them.
