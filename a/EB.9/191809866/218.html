BATH, in Hebrew antiquity

sub_entry · 213 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BATH, in Hebrew antiquity, a measure of capacity, containing the tenth part of an omar, or seven gallons and four pints, as a measure for things liquid; or three pecks and three pints, as a measure for things dry.
