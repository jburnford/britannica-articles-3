BENGO

article · 544 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BENGO, a province of the kingdom of Angola in Africa, having the sea on the west, and the province of Moschee on the east. It produces plenty of banana trees; but the Portuguese have grubbed up vast quantities of these, and cultivated the land, which now abounds with maize, and the manioc root of which they make bread*. The province is divided into a great number of districts, of which the chiefs are natives, but tributary to Portugal, and obliged to till the lands belonging to the Portuguese. They are Christians, and have eight churches.
