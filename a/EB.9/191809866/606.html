BENDS

article · 334 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BENDS, in a ship, the same with what is called twails, or wales: the utmost timbers of a ship's side, on which men set their feet in climbing up. They are reckoned from the water, and are called the first, second, or third bend. They are the chief strength of a ship's sides; and have the beams, knees, and foot-hooks, bolted to them.
