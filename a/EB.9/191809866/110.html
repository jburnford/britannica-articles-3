BASALTES

Fig. 1.

A detailed geological engraving showing a cross-section of a cliff face. The cliff is composed of numerous horizontal layers of basalt, which are depicted with vertical hatching to indicate their texture. The layers are of varying thicknesses and are separated by distinct horizontal bedding planes. In the foreground, a small group of people is gathered on the shore, some standing and some sitting, near a small boat. The sky is filled with large, billowing clouds.

Fig. 2.

An engraving of a coastal landscape. In the foreground, a group of people is on a sandy beach, engaged in fishing. One person is standing and casting a line, while others are seated or kneeling nearby. In the middle ground, several tall, conical sea stacks rise from the sea. The sea is calm with gentle ripples. The sky is filled with soft, textured clouds.
