BIDENTAL

article · 340 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BIDENTAL, in Roman Antiquity, a place blasted with lightning; which was immediately consecrated by an haruspex, with the sacrifice of a bidens. This place was afterwards accounted sacred, and it was unlawful to enter it or to tread upon it; for which reason it was commonly surrounded with a ditch, wall, hedge, ropes, &c. See next article.
