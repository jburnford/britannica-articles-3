BERSELLO

article · 255 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

BERSELLO, a fortified town of Italy in the Modenese. It was taken by Prince Eugene in 1702; and by the French in 1703, who were obliged to abandon it in 1707. It is seated near the confluence of the rivers Linza and Po, in E. Long. 10. 30. N. Lat. 44. 55.
