BELT, the Great, a famous strait of Denmark between the island of Zeeland and that of Funen, at the entrance of the Baltic sea. It is not however so commodious, nor so much frequented, as the Sound. In 1658 the whole strait was frozen so hard, that Charles Gustavus king of Sweden marched over it with a design to take Copenhagen.
