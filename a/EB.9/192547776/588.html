COALLIER

article · 368 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

COALLIER, a vessel employed to carry coals from one port to another, chiefly from the northern parts of England to the capital, and more southerly parts, as well as to foreign markets. This trade is known to be an excellent nursery for seamen, although they are often found, from the constitution of their climate, not to be so well calculated for southern navigation.
