COMUM

COMUM, in Ancient Geography, a town of the Orobii, of an ancient standing, and formerly powerful, daring to dispute with the Romans: Comenses, the people; Comensis Ager, the epithet. It became afterwards no inconsiderable municipium, to which Julius Cæsar added 5000 new colonists (Strabo); whence it was generally called Novocomum, and the people Novocomenses. But in time it recovered its ancient name, Comum; Pliny the younger, a native of that place, calling it by no other name. Now Como, in the duchy of Milan, at the south end of the lake of that name. E. Long. 9. 37. N. Lat. 46. It is about 80 miles N. E. of Turin.
