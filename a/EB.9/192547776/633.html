COCKLES, PUB. HORATIUS, a celebrated Roman, who alone opposed the whole army of Porfenna at the head of a bridge, while his companions behind him were cutting off the communication with the other shore. When the bridge was destroyed, Cockles though wounded by the darts of the enemy, leapt into the Tiber, and swam across it with his arms,

A brazen statue was raised to him in the temple of Vulcan, by the consul Publicola, for his eminent services.
