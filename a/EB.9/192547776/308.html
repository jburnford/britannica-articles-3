CIRTA, in Ancient Geography, the metropolis and royal residence, not far from the river Ampelaga, in the inland parts of Numidia Propera. A colony, furnished Colonia Sittianorum, very rich, when in the hands of Syphax. The colony was led by one P. Sittius, under the auspices of Cæsar, and was furnished Julia. Now called Constantina, in Algiers. E. Long. 7. 0. N. Lat. 35. 30.
