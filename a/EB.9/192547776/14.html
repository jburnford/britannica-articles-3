One Very Large Volume 8vo. 15s. boards. * * * This Work explains not only the form of Lease, but the rights arising from it both to landlord and tenant, as well as the rights held by them respectively at common law; with reference to the authorities and decisions by which these have been fixed. In general, the decisions are given so fully, as to render any reference to the original division unnecessary, and of course supplying to the country practitioner, the place of an extensive library on this head.—The Publishers can therefore recommend this publication to the man of business in the country, as containing a great body of Law on that department of his profession, certainly the most important to him, and of the most frequent occurrence; and to the country gentleman and farmer, as affording information to them on a subject in which their interests, or the interests of their families, are more materially concerned, than in any other within the whole compass of the law. The present edition has been considerably improved by introduction of all the late cases, and by a table of contents and index, which must give the reader a complete view, and ready and easy access to the whole subjects treated of.
