CLAVI VESTIUM

article · 324 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CLAVI VESTIUM, were flowers or studs of purple, interwoven with or sewed upon the garments of knights or senators; only, for distinction, the former used them narrow, the latter broad.

CLAVIS properly signifies a KEY; and is sometimes used in English to denote an explanation of some obscure passages of any book or writing.
