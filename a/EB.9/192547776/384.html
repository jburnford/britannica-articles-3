CLARKE, Samuel, the son of the former, was fellow of Pembroke-hall in Cambridge; but was ejected from his fellowship for refusing to take the engagements, as he was also afterwards from his rectory of Grendon in Buckinghamshire. He applied himself early to the study of the Scriptures, and his Annotations on the Bible, printed together with the sacred text, is highly commended.

Clarke. commended by Dr Owen, Mr Baxter, and Dr Calamy. He died in 1701, aged 75.
