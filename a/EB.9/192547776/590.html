COANE

article · 420 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

COANE, among the Greeks, a name given to a peculiar species of tutia or tutty, which was always found in a tubular form. It had its name from xan, a word used to express a sort of cylindrical tube, into which the melted brass was received from the furnace, and in which it was suffered to cool. In cooling, it always deposited a sort of cretaceous on the sides of the vessel or tube, and this was the tutty called coane.
