COCCULUS INDICUS

article · 460 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

COCCULUS INDICUS, the name of a poisonous berry, too frequently mixed with malt-liquors in order to make them intoxicating; but this practice is expressly forbidden by act of parliament. It is the fruit of the MANISPERMUM Cocculus. Fishermen have a way of mixing it with paste, which the fish swallow greedily, and are thereby rendered lifeless for a time, and float on the water. It is sometimes used with slaveface, for destroying vermin in children's heads.
