CHOROIDES

article · 820 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CHOROIDES, or CHOROIDES in Anatomy, a term applied to several parts of the body, bearing some resemblance to the chorion. The word is formed from chorion, chorion, and ades, likeness.

CHOROIDES is particularly used for the inner membrane which immediately invests the brain; so called as being intermingled with a great number of blood-vessels, like the chorion: but more usually denominated the pia mater, or membrane tenuis.

Plexus or Locus CHOROIDES, is a knot of veins and arteries in the anterior ventricle of the brain, woven out of the branches of the carotid.

CHOROIDES is also applied to the inner and posterior tunic of the eye, immediately under the sclerotic. It is soft, thin, and black; and its inner or concave surface is very smooth and polished. It has its name from its being interspersed with vessels.
