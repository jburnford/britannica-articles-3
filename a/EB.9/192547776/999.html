COMPEIGNE

article · 304 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

COMPEIGNE, a handsome town of France, in the department of Oise, with a palace, or castle, where the king often resided. The maid of Orleans was taken prisoner here in 1430. It is seated on the river Oise, near a large forest. E. Long. 2. 55. N. Lat. 49. 25. It stands about 45 miles north-east of Paris.
