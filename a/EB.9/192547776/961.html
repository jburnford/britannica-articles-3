COMMUNIBUS LOCIS

article · 688 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

COMMUNIBUS LOCIS, a Latin term, in frequent use among philosophical, &c. writers; implying some medium, or mean relation, between several places. De Keil supposes the ocean to be one quarter of a mile deep, communibus locis, q. d. at a medium, or taking one place with another.

Communibus Annis, has the same import with regard to years that communibus locis has with regard to places. Mr Derham observes that the depth of rain, communibus annis, or one year with another, were it to stagnate on the earth, would amount in Townley in Lancashire, to 42\frac{1}{2} inches; at Upminster in Essex, to 19\frac{1}{2}; at Zurich, 32\frac{1}{2}; at Pisa, 43\frac{1}{2}; and at Paris to 19 inches.
