COIX, JOHNS-TEARS. See BOTANY INDEX. In Spain and Portugal the poor people grind the seeds of this plant in times of scarcity, and make a coarse kind of bread of them. The seeds are inclosed in small capsules about the bigness of an English pea, and of different colours. These are strung upon silk, and used instead of bracelets by some of the poorer sort in the West Indies, but especially by the negroes.
