CISSA

article · 332 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

CISSA, or CISSUM, in Ancient Geography, a town of the Hither Spain, in Læcetania, on the east side of the Iberus, (thought to be Guifona) where the Carthaginians were first defeated by Scipio. Another Cissa of Thrace, situated on the river Agos Potamus, which Scylax seems to call Cressa, or Crisso; so that the reading is doubtful.
