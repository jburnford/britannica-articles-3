CNOSSUS, or CNOSUS, anciently called Carratas, from a cognominal river running by it; a city of Crete, 23 miles to the east of Gortina (Peutinger). Here stood the sepulchre of Jupiter, the famous labyrinth, and the palace of Minos, a very ancient king; here happened the adventure of Ariadne his daughter with Theseus, called Gnost (Ovid). Its port-town was Heracleum, on the east side of the island.
