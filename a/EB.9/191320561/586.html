FOLLICULUS

article · 617 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

FOLLICULUS, (from follis, "a bag,") a species of feed-vessel first mentioned by Linnaeus in his Delineatio Plantae, generally consisting of one valve, which opens from bottom to top on one side, and has no future for fastening or attaching the seeds within it.

FOLLICULI are likewise defined by the same author to be small glandular vessels distended with air, which appear on the surface of some plants; as at the root of water-milfoil, and on the leaves of aldrovanda. In the former, the vessels in question are roundish, and furnished with an appearance like two horns; in the latter, pot-shaped, and femicircular.
