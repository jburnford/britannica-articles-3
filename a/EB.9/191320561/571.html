FOLC-LANDS, (Sax.) copyhold lands so called in the time of the Saxons, as charter-lands were called boe-lands, Kitch. 174. Folkland was terra vulgi or popularis; the land of the vulgar people, who had no certain estate therein, but held the same, under the rents and services accustomed or agreed, at the will only of their lord the thane; and it was therefore not put in writing, but accounted prædium rusticum et ignobile. Spelm. of Feuds, c. 5.
