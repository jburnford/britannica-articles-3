FALLOPIAN TUBES

FALLOPIAN TUBES, in Anatomy, two ducts arising from the womb, one on each side of the fundus, and thence extended to the ovaries, having a considerable share in conception. They are called tubæ, from their form, which bears some resemblance to a trumpet; and their denomination Fallopianæ, they take

from Gabriel Fallopius, mentioned in the next article. Fallopius See ANATOMY Index.
