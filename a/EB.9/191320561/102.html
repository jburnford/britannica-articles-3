FASTERMANS, or FASTING-MEN

sub_entry · 263 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

FASTERMANS, or FASTING-MEN, q. d. homines habentes, was used in our ancient customs for men in repute and substance; or rather for pledges, sureties, or bondsmen, who, according to the Saxon polity, were fast bound to answer for one another's peaceable behaviour.
