FOP

article · 236 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

FOP, probably derived from the vappa of Horace, applied in the first satire of his first book to the wild and extravagant Nævius, is used among us to denote a person who cultivates a regard to adventitious ornament and beauty to excess.
