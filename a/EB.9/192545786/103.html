MANCIPLE

article · 374 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MANCIPLE (mancipr), a clerk of the kitchen, or caterer. An officer in the inner temple was anciently so called, who is now the steward there; of whom Chaucer, the ancient English poet, some time a student of that house, thus writes:

A manciple there was within the temple,
Of which all caterers might take ensample.

This officer still remains in colleges in the universities.
