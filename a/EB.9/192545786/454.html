MASTER OF THE HORSE

article · 1,059 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MASTER OF THE HORSE is reckoned the third great officer of the court, and is an office of great honour and antiquity, and always (when not put in commission), filled by noblemen of the highest rank and abilities. He has the management and disposal of all the king's stables and bred horses. He has authority over the equestrians and pages, coachmen, footmen, grooms, riders of the great horse, farriers, and smiths. He appoints all the other tradesmen who work for the king's stables; and by his warrant to the avenor, makes them give an oath to be true and faithful. In short, he is intrusted with all the lands and revenues appropriated for the king's breed of horses, the expenses of the stable, and of the coaches, litters, &c. He alone has the privilege of making use of any of the king's horses, pages, footmen, &c.; and at any solemn cavalcade he rides next the king, and leads a horse of state. His salary is 1276l. 13s. 4d. per annum. There is also a master of the horse in the establishment of her majesty's household, with a salary of 800l. a-year.
