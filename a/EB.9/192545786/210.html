MARALDI

MARALDI, JAMES-PHILIP, a learned mathematician and astronomer, of the Academy of Sciences at Paris, was born in 1665. He was the son of Francis Maraldi and Angela Catharine Caffini, the sister of the famous astronomer of that name. His uncle made him go to France in 1687, where he acquired great reputation on account of his learning and observations. He made a catalogue of the fixed stars, which is more particular and exact than Bayer's; and has given a great number of curious and interesting observations in the memoirs of the academy; in particular, those on bees and petrifications have been universally applauded. He died in 1729.
