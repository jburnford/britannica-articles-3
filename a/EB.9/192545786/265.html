MARGATE

MARGATE, a sea-port town of Kent, on the north side of the isle of Thanet, near the North Foreland. It is noted for shipping vast quantities of corn (most, if not all, the product of that island) for London; and has a salt-water bath at the post-house, which has performed great cures in nervous and paralytic cases, and numbness of the limbs. It lies in St John's parish, which is a member of the port of Dover, at the distance of 14 miles, 12 from Canterbury, and 72 from London; and in the summer season is frequented for sea-bathing, having become one of the principal watering places for the idle, the opulent, and the invalid, where they meet with every requisite accommodation; and the adjacent country abounds with most extensive prospects and pleasant rides. E. Long. 1. 30. N. Lat. 51. 24.
