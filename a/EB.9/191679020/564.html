LARGE

article · 545 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

LARGE, a sea term applied to the wind when it crosses the line of a ship's course in a favourable direction, particularly on the beam or quarter. Thus, if a ship steer west, then the wind in any point of the compass to the eastward of the south or north may be called large, unless when it is directly east, and then it is said to be right ast. Sailing large is, therefore, advancing with a large wind, so as that the sheets are slackened and flowing, and the bow lines entirely disused. This phrase is generally opposed to sailing close hauled.
