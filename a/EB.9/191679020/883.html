LEUCA

article · 279 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

LEUCA, in antiquity, a geographical measure of length in use among the latter Gauls; which, according to Jornandes, who calls it leuga, contained fifteen hundred paces, or one mile and a half. Hence the name of league, now reckoned at three miles; in the lower age, called leuga.
