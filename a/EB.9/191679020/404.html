LÆLIUS, CAIUS

article · 480 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

LÆLIUS, CAIUS, a Roman consul and great orator, furnished the Wife, distinguished himself in Spain in the war against Viriathus the Spanish general. He is highly praised by Cicero, who gives an admirable description of the intimate friendship which subsisted between Lælius and Scipio Africanus the Younger. His eloquence, his modesty, and his abilities, acquired him a great reputation; and he is thought to have assisted Terence in his comedies. He died about the year 126 B. C.
