LARENTINALIA

article · 303 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

LARENTINALIA, in antiquity, a feast held among the Romans on the 23d day of December, but ordered to be observed twice a year by Augustus; by some supposed to have been in honour of the Lares, but by others, with more probability, in honour of Acca Laurentia; and to have been the same with Laurentalia.
