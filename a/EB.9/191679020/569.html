LARISSA

article · 446 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

LARISSA, an ancient, rich, and celebrated town of Greece, in the province of Janna or Thessaly, with an archbishop's see of the Greek church, a palace, and several handsome mosques. According to Virgil, it was the country of Achilles. It was also the place where Philip the father of Alexander the Great resided. —The inhabitants carry on a considerable trade. The city is agreeably seated on the river Peneus, in E. Long. 23. 36. N. Lat. 38. 51.
