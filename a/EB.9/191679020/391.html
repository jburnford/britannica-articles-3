LACYDES

article · 712 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

LACYDES, a Greek philosopher, born at Cyrene, was the disciple of Arcefilas, and his successor in the academy. He taught in a garden given him by Attalus king of Pergamus; but that prince sending for him to court, he replied, "That the pictures of kings should be viewed at a distance." He imitated his master in the pleasure he took in doing good without caring to have it known: he had a goose which followed him everywhere by night as well as by day; and when she died, he made a funeral for her, which was as magnificent as if it had been for a son or a brother. He taught the same doctrine as Arcefilas; and pretended that we ought to determine nothing, but always to suspend our opinion. He died 212. B. C.
