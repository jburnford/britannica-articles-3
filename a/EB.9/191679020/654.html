LAUS POMPEIA

LAUS POMPEIA, in Ancient Geography, a town of Infubria, situated to the east of Milan, between the rivers Addua and Lamber. A town built by the Boii after their passing the Alps: its ancient Gaulic name is unknown. Strabo Pompeius, father of Pompey, leading thither a colony, gave it a new name, and conferred the Jus Latii on the ancient inhabitants who remained there. The modern Lodi is built from its ruins, at some distance off. E. Long. 10. 15. N. Lat. 45. 22.
