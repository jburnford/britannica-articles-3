JUSTIFICATION, in Theology, that act of grace which renders a man just in the sight of God, and worthy of eternal happiness. See THEOLOGY.

The Romanists and Reformers are extremely divided about the doctrine of justification; the latter contending for justification by faith alone, and the former by good works.
