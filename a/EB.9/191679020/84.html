KARLE

article · 233 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

KARLE, a Saxon word used in our law, sometimes simply for a man; and sometimes, with an addition, for a servant or clown. Thus the Saxons call a seaman busearl, and a domestic servant busearle. From hence comes the modern word charl.
