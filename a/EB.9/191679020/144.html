KERN, or KERNE, a term in the ancient Irish mili-
tia, signifying a foi soldier. Camden tells us, the ar-
mies of Ireland consisted of cavalry, called galloglasses;
and infantry, lightly armed, called kernes.—The kernes
bore swords and darts; to the last were fitted cords, by
which they could recover them after they had been
launched out.
