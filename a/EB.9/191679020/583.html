LASSITUDE

LASSIDUDE, or WEARINESS, in Medicine, a morbid sensation, that comes on spontaneously, without any previous motion, exercise, or labour. This is a

frequent symptom in acute distempers: it arises either from an increase of bulk, a diminution of proper evacuation, or too great a consumption of the fluids necessary to maintain the spring of the solids, or from a vitiated secretion of that juice.
