LERIDA

article · 385 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

LERIDA, an ancient, strong, and large town of Spain, in Catalonia, with a bishop's see, an university, and a strong castle. This place declared for King Charles after the reduction of Barcelona in 1705; but it was retaken by the duke of Orleans in 1707, after the battle of Almanza. It is seated on a hill near the river Segra, and in a fertile soil, in E. Long. 0. 35. N. Lat. 41. 31.
