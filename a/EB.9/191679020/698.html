LEAPING

article · 651 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

LEAPING, or VAULTING, was an exercise much used both amongst the Greeks and Romans. The Grecians called it Αλμα, and performed it with weights upon their heads and shoulders. Sometimes they carried the weights in their hands, which were of different figures, but generally oval and made with holes or covered with thongs, through which the contenders put their fingers. These weights were called ἀλματι. The contest was who could leap the highest and farthest. The place from whence they jumped was called βαρες, and that to which they leaped, σκαμυνα, because the ground was there dug up. This exercise was performed in the same manner by the Romans.
