FRESH WATER

article · 474 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

FRESH WATER, is that not tinctured or impregnated with salt or saline particles enough to be discovered by the sense. Such generally is that of springs, rains, wells, lakes, &c.

The dulcifying or making of salt water fresh is a secret that has been long sought with great attention. For an account of the principal attempts that have been made with this view. See Sea Water.

Fresh Wind signifies strong, but not violent; hence when the gale increases, it is said to freshen.
