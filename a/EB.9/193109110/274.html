FRODSHAM

article · 541 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

FRODSHAM, a town of Cheshire in England, 162 miles from London, is noted for its ancient castle. It has a stone bridge over the river Weaver near its conflux with the Mersey, and a harbour for ships of good burden. By means of inland navigation, it has communication with the rivers Dee, Ribble, Ouse, Trent, Darwent, Severn, Humber, Thames, Avon, &c. which navigation, including its windings, extends above 500 miles, in the counties of Lincoln, Nottingham, York, Lancaster, Westmorland, Stafford, Warwick, Leicester, Oxford, Worcester, &c.
