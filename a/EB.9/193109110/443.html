GALACTOPHAGI

article · 524 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

GALACTOPHAGI, and GALACTOPOTÆ, in antiquity, persons who lived wholly on milk, without corn or the use of any other food. The words are compounded of γαλα, γαλακτες, milk; φασι, to eat; and πότης of πίνω, I drink.

Certain nations in Scythia Asiatica, as the Getæ, Nomades, &c. are famous, in ancient history, in quality of galactophagi, or milk-eaters. Homer makes their eloge, Ἰλιάδ. lib. iii.

Ptolemy, in his geography, places the Galactophagi between the Rhipæan mountains on one side, and the Hyrcanian sea on the other.
