GALEA

article · 384 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

GALEA, in antiquity, a light casque, head piece, or morion, coming down to the shoulders, and commonly of brass; though Camillus, according to Plutarch, ordered those of his army to be of iron, as being the stronger metal. The lower part of it was called bucula, and on the top was a crest. The velites wore a light galea, made of the skin of some wild beast to make it more terrible.
