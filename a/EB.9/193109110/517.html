GALLON, a measure of capacity both for dry and liquid things, containing four quarts. But these quarts, and consequently the gallon itself, are different, according to the quality of the things measured: For instance, the wine gallon contains 231 cubic inches, and holds eight pounds avoirdupois of pure water; the beer and ale gallon contains 282 solid inches, and holds ten pounds three ounces and a quarter avoirdupois of water; and the gallon for corn, meal, &c. 27 2 1/2 cubic inches, and holds nine pounds thirteen ounces of pure water.
