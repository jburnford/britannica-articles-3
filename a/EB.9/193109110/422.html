GÆTULIA

article · 479 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

GÆTULIA, in Ancient Geography, a country of Africa, lying to the south of Mauritania, called Gætulia Propria, and Vetus. Gætuli, the people, were distinguished by different epithets; as Nigri, Autololes, Daræ and Baniuræ, (Pliny). The Gætuli were among the first inhabitants of Africa; a rough, unpolished people, living on venison and the spontaneous productions of the earth; a roving, wandering people, who took up with the first place in which might surprise them, (Sallust).
