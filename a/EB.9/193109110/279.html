FRONT

article · 457 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

FRONT, the forehead, or that part of the face above the eyebrows. The word is formed of the Latin front; and that from the Greek φρονεῖν, "to think, perceive;" of φροῦναι, "the mind, thought." Martinus, to make out this etymology, observes, that from the forehead of a person we perceive what he is, what he is capable of, and what he thinks of.

FRONT is also used where several persons or things are ranged side by side, and show their front or fore parts.
