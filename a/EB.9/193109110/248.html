FRIEDENSHUETTEN

article · 368 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

FRIEDENSHUETTEN, a Moravian settlement whose name signifies tents of peace, situated on the Susquehannah river in Pennsylvania, about 24 miles below Tioga point, which owed its origin to the united brethren, in the year 1765. At that period it contained 13 huts belonging to the Indians, besides 40 houses constructed after the European manner, and a very neat chapel.
