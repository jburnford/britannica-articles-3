PURITAN

article · 389 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PURITAN, a name formerly given in derision to the dissenters from the church of England, on account of the profession to follow the pure word of God, in opposition to all traditions and human constitutions. It was likewise given in the primitive church to the Novatian schismatics, because they would never admit to communion any one who from dread of death had apostatized from the faith.
