PROPOSITION

PROPOSITION, in Mathematics, is either some truth advanced and shown to be such by demonstration, or some operation proposed and its solution shown. If the

Proposition proposition be deduced from several theoretical definitions compared together, it is called a theorem; if from a praxis, or series of operations, it is called a problem. See the articles THEOREM and PROBLEM.
