PUNCTUM SALIENS

article · 416 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

PUNCTUM SALIENS, in Anatomy, the first rudiments of the heart in the formation of the fetus, where a throbbing motion is perceived. This is said to be easily observed with a microscope in a brood-egg, wherein after conception, we see a little speck or cloud, in the middle whereof is a spot that appears to beat or leap a considerable time before the fetus is formed for hatching.
