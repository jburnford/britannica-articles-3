RHEGIUM

article · 616 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

RHEGIUM, in Ancient Geography, so very ancient a city as to be supposed to take its name from the violent bursting of the coast of Italy from Sicily, thought to have been formerly conjoined (Mela, Virgil). A city of the Bruttii, a colony of Chalcidians from Eutrea: a strong barrier opposed to Sicily (Strabo); mentioned by Luke; furnished Siculum (Ptolemy), from a fresh supply of inhabitants sent hither by Augustus, after driving Sextus Pompeius out of Sicily (Strabo); and thus was in part a colony, retaining still the right of a municipium (Inscription). The city is now called Reggio, in the Farther Calabria.
