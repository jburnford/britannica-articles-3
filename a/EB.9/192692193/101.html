HETERIARCH

article · 494 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

HETERIARCH, HETERIARCHA, in antiquity, an officer in the Greek empire, whereof there were two species; the one called simply heteriarch, and the other great heteriarch, who had the direction of the former.

The word is Greek, ἱταρχία, formed of the Greek ἱταρχός, "companion, ally," and ἄρχω, "command." Their principal function was to command the troops of the allies; besides which, they had some other duties in the emperor's court, described by Codin, De Officiis, cap. 5. No 30, 31, 32, 37.
