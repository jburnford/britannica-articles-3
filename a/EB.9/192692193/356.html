HOOKER

HOOKER, in naval architecture, a vessel much used by the Dutch, built like a pink, but rigged and masted like a hoy.—Hookers will lie nearer a wind than vessels with cross-fails can do. They are from 50 to 200 tons burden, and with a few hands will sail to the East Indies.
