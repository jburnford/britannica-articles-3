HORDICALIA

article · 431 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

HORDICALIA, or HORDIEMIDIA, in antiquity, a religious feast held among the Romans, wherein they sacrificed cattle big with young. This feast fell on April 15. on which day they sacrificed 30 cows with calf to the goddess Tellus or the Earth; part of them were sacrificed in the temple of Jupiter. The calves taken out of their bellies were burnt to allies at first by the pontifices, afterwards by the eldest of the vestal virgins.
