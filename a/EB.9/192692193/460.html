HOUSING

article · 288 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

HOUSING, or Houss-Line, in the sea-language, a small line, formed of three fine strands or twists of hemp, smaller than rope-yarn. It is chiefly used to seize blocks into their slots, to bind the corners of the sails, or to fasten the bottom of a sail to its bolt-rope, &c. See Bolt-Rope.
