HEXAMETER

article · 301 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

HEXAMETER, in ancient poetry, a kind of verse consisting of six feet; the first four of which may be indifferently either spondees or dactyles; the fifth is generally a dactyl, and the sixth always a spondee. Such is the following verse of Horace:

1 2 3 4 5 6
Aut prolesse volūti, aut delictare poterat
