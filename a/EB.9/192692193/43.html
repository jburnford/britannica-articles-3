HERMIONE

HERMIONE, in Ancient Geography, a considerable city of Argolis. It was in ruins (except a few temples) in the time of Paulanias; who says that the new city was at the distance of four stadia from the promontory on which the temple of Neptune stood. It gave name to the Sinus Hermionicus, a part of the Sinus Argolicus.
