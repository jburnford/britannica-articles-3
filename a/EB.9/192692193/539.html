HURST

article · 280 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

HURST, HYRST, or HERST, are derived from the Saxon hyrð, i. e. a wood, or grove of trees. There are many places in Kent, Sussex, and Hampshire, which begin and end with this syllable; and the reason may be, because the great wood called Andreswald extended through those counties.
