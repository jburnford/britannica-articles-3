HERITAGE

article · 323 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

HERITAGE, in Scotts Law, lands, houses, or any immovable subject, in contradistinction to moveables or moveable subjects. It also sometimes signifies such immovable property as a person succeeds to as heir to another, in contradistinction to that which he himself purchases or acquires in any other manner, called conquest.
