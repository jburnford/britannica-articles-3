MITHRIDATE

MITHRIDATE, an old term, in Pharmacy; an antidote, or composition, in form of an electuary, supposed to serve either as a remedy or a preservative against poisons. It takes its name from the inventor, Mithridates king of Pontus, who is said to have so fortified his body against poisons with antidotes and preservatives, that when he had a mind to despatch himself, he could not find any poison that would take effect.
