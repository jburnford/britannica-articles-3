MISSIO

article · 250 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MISSIO, among the Romans, was a full discharge given to a soldier after 20 years service, and differed from the exvocatorio, which was a discharge from duty after 17 years service. Every soldier had a right to claim his missio at the end of 20 years.
