MISSUS

article · 355 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MISSUS, in the Circensian games, were the matches in horse or chariot races. The usual number of missus or matches in one day was 243; though the emperor Domitian presented the people with 100. The last match was generally made at the expence of the people, who made a collection for the purpose; hence it was called missus aerarius, a subscription plate.
