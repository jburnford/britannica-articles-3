MONOGYNIA

article · 297 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MONOGYNIA, from monos, alone, and gynē, a woman; the name of the first order or subdivision in the first 13 classes of Linnaeus's sexual method; consisting of plants which, besides their agreement in their classic character, generally derived from the number of their stamens, have only one style.
