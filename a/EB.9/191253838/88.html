MINAGNGHINIM

article · 368 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

MINAGNGHINIM, a pulsative instrument of music, among the Hebrews, which was a square table of wood, fitted with a handle; over this table was stretched

stretched an iron chain, or hempen cord, passing through balls of wood or brass, which struck against the table, when the instrument was shaken, and occasioned a clear sound, which might be heard at a great distance.
