SALTIER, one of the honourable ordinaries.—See HERALDRY.

This, says G. Leigh, in his Accedence of Arms, p. 70, was anciently made of the height of a man, and driven full of pins, the use of which was to scale walls, &c. Upton says it was an instrument to catch wild beasts, whence he derives this word from faltur, i. e. a foreit." The French call this ordinary sautour, from sauter "to leap;" because it may have been used by soldiers to leap over walls of towns, which in former times were but low; but some modern authors think it is borne in imitation of St Andrew's cross.
