SALVE REGINA

article · 494 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

SALVE REGINA, among the Romanists, the name of a Latin prayer, addressed to the Virgin, and sung after complines, as also upon the point of executing a criminal. Durandus says, it was composed by Peter bishop of Compostella. The custom of singing the salve regina at the close of the office was begun by order of St. Dominic, and first in the congregation of Dominicans at Bologna, about 1237. Gregory IX. first appointed it to be general. St. Bernard added the conclusion, O dulcis! O pia, &c.
