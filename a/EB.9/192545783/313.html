ANDOVER

ANDOVER, a large market town in Hampshire, which is situated on a branch of the river Test. It has several inns, which afford good accommodation for travellers; and has a market on Saturday, well stocked with provisions. It is governed by a bailiff, a steward, a recorder, ten approved men, and twenty-two capital burghesses, who yearly choose the bailiff, and he elects two sergeants at mace to attend him. The living is a vicarage, valued at 1711. 48. 4d. in the king's books. W. Long. 0. 56. N. Lat. 51. 20.
