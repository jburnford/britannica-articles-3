ANOMOEANS

article · 757 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ANOMOEANS, in Ecclesiastical History, the name by which the pure Arians were called in the fourth century, in contradistinction to the Semi-Arians. The word is formed from the Greek, anomos, different, dis-

similar: For the pure Arians asserted, that the Semi-Arians was of a nature different from, and in nothing like, that of the Father: whereas the Semi-Arians acknowledged a likeness of nature in the Son; at the same time that they denied, with the pure Arians, the consubstantiality of the Word. The Semi-Arians condemned the Anomoeans in the council of Seleucia; and the Anomoeans in their turn condemned the Semi-Arians in the councils of Constantinople and Antioch, erasing the word ous, like, out of the Formula of Rimini and that of Constantinople.
