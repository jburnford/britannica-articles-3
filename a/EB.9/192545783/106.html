AMPLIATION

article · 287 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

AMPLIATION, in Roman Antiquity, was the deferring to a sentence in certain causes. This the judge did, by pronouncing the word amplius; or by writing the letters N. I., for non liquet; thereby signifying, that, as the cause was not clear, it would be necessary to bring further evidence.
