AMPHORA

article · 315 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

AMPHORA, in Antiquity, a liquid measure among the Greeks and Romans. The Roman amphora contained 48 sextaries, equal to about seven gallons one pint English wine measure; and the Grecian or Attic amphora contained one-third more.

AMPHORA was also a dry measure used by the Romans, and contained about three bushels.
