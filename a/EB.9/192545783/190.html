ANAK

article · 650 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ANAK, the father of the Anakims, was the son of Arba, who gave his name to Kirjath-arba, or Hebron, (Josh. xiv. 15.) Anak had three sons, Shefami, Ahiman, and Talmi, (chap. xv. 14. and Numb. xiii. 22.), who, as well as their father, were giants, and who, with their posterity, all terrible for their fierceness and extraordinary stature, were called the Anakims; in compassion of whom the Hebrews, who were sent to view the land of Canaan, reported that they were but as grasshoppers, Numb. xiii. ult. Caleb, assisted by the tribe of Judah, took Kirjath-arba, and destroyed

the Anakims (Judges i. 20. and Josh. xv. 14.) in the year of the world 2559.
