ANACRISIS

article · 500 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ANACRISIS, among the Ancient Greeks, was used for a kind of trial or examination, which the archons, or chief magistrates of Athens, were to undergo before their admission into that office. The anacrisis stands distinguished from the doximasia, which was a second examination in the forum. The anacrisis was performed in the senate-house. The questions here proposed to them were concerning their family, kindred, behaviour, estate, &c. Some will have it that all magistrates underwent the anacrisis.
