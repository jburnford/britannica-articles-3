ANACHRONISM

article · 466 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ANACHRONISM, in Matters of Literature, an error with respect to chronology, whereby an event is placed earlier than it really happened.—The word is compounded of ana, "higher," and chronos, "time." Such is that of Virgil, who placed Dido in Africa at the time of Aeneas, though in reality she did not come there till 300 years after the taking of Troy.—An error on the other side, whereby a fact is placed later and lower than it should be, is called a parachronism.
