ANDRISCUS

article · 689 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ANDRISCUS, a man of mean extraction, who, pretending to be the son of Perseus last king of Macedonia, took upon him the name of Philip, for which reason he was called Pseudo-Philippus, the Faise Philip. After a complete victory over Juventus, the Roman praetor sent against him, he assumed kingly power, but exercised it with vast cruelty. At last, the Romans obliged him to fly into Thrace, where he was betrayed and delivered into the hands of Metellus. This victory gained Macedonia once more into the power of the Romans, and to Metellus the name of Macedonicus, but cost the Romans 25,000 men. Andriscus adorned the triumph of Metellus, walking in chains before the general's chariot.
