ANNALIS CLAVUS

article · 298 chars · lineage ↗ · page image at NLS ↗

ANNALIS CLAVUS, the nail which the praetor, consul, or dictator, drove into the wall of Jupiter's temple annually upon the ides of September, to show the number of years. But this custom was superseded by reckoning years by consulships. The ceremony was sometimes performed to avert the plague, &c.
