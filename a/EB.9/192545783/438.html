ANIME

ANIME, a resin exuding from the trunk of a large American tree, called by Pifo jetauba, by the Indians courbaril, (a species of HYMENEA). This resin is of a transparent amber colour, a light agreeable smell, and little or no taste. It dissolves entirely, but not very readily, in rectified spirit of wine; the impurities, which are often in large quantity, remaining behind. The Brazilians are said to employ anime in fumigations for pains and aches proceeding from a cold cause: with us, it is rarely, if ever, made use of for any medicinal purposes.
